# NRPG — Movement Positioning Document

---

## The Manifesto Opening

You are a professional. You have certifications, equipment, liability, staff, and a decade of hard-won knowledge. And yet the system you operate inside treats you like a subcontractor — tells you what to charge, tells you how to document it, and takes a margin for the privilege of sending you the job. That is not a partnership. That is capture. NRPG exists because ANZ restorers deserve something better than the arrangement they inherited — and because the tools, the community, and the moment to change it are finally here.

---

## What NRPG Stands For

**National Restoration Practitioners Group.**

But the acronym is the least important part.

NRPG stands for the belief that the restoration contractor is a professional — not a vendor, not a preferred supplier number, not a managed repair unit. A professional with expertise, responsibility, and legitimate commercial interests that are worth defending.

NRPG stands for the conviction that the ANZ restoration industry does not have to operate the way it currently operates. That managed repair dependency is not a permanent condition. That independent operators can build businesses that are profitable, scalable, and sovereign — serving property owners directly, on terms they set.

NRPG stands for the idea that community accelerates what technology enables. That the fastest way to change an industry is to connect the operators who are already thinking this way, give them the framework and the tools, and let them move together.

This is not a union. It is not a lobby group. It is not a trade association.

It is a practitioner community built on a business model — the belief that the restorer who invests in systems, education, and professional development will outcompete the one who stays in the program. And that when enough operators make that shift, the dynamics of the entire industry change.

---

## The Problem NRPG Solves

ANZ restorers are operating five years behind the curve.

In the United States, the independent restoration movement emerged from exactly the same dynamics. Managed repair programs, insurer-directed work, margin compression, contractor dependency. The operators who saw it clearly built their way out — through professional development, direct property owner relationships, documented capability, and refusal to compete on price alone.

The community infrastructure that supported that shift in the US — the education, the peer networks, the practitioner platforms — is mature and proven. The model works.

In ANZ, that infrastructure does not yet exist at scale. Operators are solving the same problems in isolation, making the same mistakes, and having the same fights with the same programs — without the benefit of a community that has been through it.

NRPG is the infrastructure. Built for ANZ, informed by the global movement, designed for the specific dynamics of the Australian and New Zealand market.

The problem is not that ANZ restorers lack skill. The problem is that skilled operators are working without the systems, the community, or the framework to turn their skill into a business that doesn't depend on managed repair programs for its survival.

NRPG solves that.

## Three Pillars of NRPG Membership

### 1. Community

The peer group you cannot build in isolation.

NRPG connects independent ANZ restoration operators who are at the same inflection point — capable businesses that are ready to build past owner-dependency, managed repair reliance, and survival-mode pricing. Peer forums, working groups, shared case studies, and direct access to operators who have solved the problems you're currently facing.

Community is not a benefit add-on. It is the mechanism. The fastest path to a better business is proximity to people who have already built one.

### 2. Education: Structure → Systems → Scale

The Restopreneur™ framework, applied to ANZ.

The education pillar is built on a single conviction: most restoration businesses fail to scale not because of market conditions, but because they never build the structural foundation that scaling requires. Structure comes first — clear business model, defined services, documented value. Systems come second — job management, compliance, team operations that run without the owner. Scale comes third — growth without chaos, leverage without burnout.

NRPG delivers this framework through practitioner education, workshops, and a curriculum built specifically for restoration business owners who are ready to do the work.

### 3. Technology: RestoreAssist + DR

The tools that make independence commercially viable.

Philosophy without infrastructure is just rhetoric. NRPG members get access to the technology stack that underpins the independent operator model:

**DR** — the operational platform that runs restoration businesses without the owner needing to be everywhere. Job management, compliance, team visibility.

**RestoreAssist** — the client-facing and insurer-interface layer that positions the contractor as a professional service provider, not a preferred supplier.

Together, these platforms give NRPG members the operational capability to compete on quality and documented performance — not just price and program access.

## Who Joins NRPG

### The ANZ Restopreneur™

The Restopreneur™ is a specific type of operator. Not every restoration contractor is one. Not every one who starts becomes one. But the profile is consistent:

- **Runs an established business** — typically 3–20 staff, $1M–$6M revenue, past the startup phase
- **Is technically excellent but operationally stretched** — the craft is there; the systems are not
- **Has a managed repair relationship they're not satisfied with** — either actively looking to reduce dependency, or aware that they should be
- **Takes professional development seriously** — reads, listens, invests in their own growth
- **Wants to build a business, not just a job** — thinks about the exit, the team, the legacy
- **Is tired of being treated like a subcontractor** — knows their value, resents the arrangement, is ready to change it

The Restopreneur™ is not defined by the size of the business. It is defined by the orientation of the operator. Someone running a $1.2M business with the right framework and the right systems is a Restopreneur™. Someone running a $4M business that still lives entirely in the owner's head is not.

## Why Now

The ANZ managed repair ecosystem is at a specific point in its development.

The programs are mature. The margin compression is established. The contractor dependency is documented and understood. Operators who have been in the industry for more than five years know exactly what the managed repair relationship costs them — in margin, in autonomy, in the ability to build a business that is genuinely theirs.

What has been missing until now is the infrastructure to do something about it.

That infrastructure is here.

NRPG provides the community framework. DR provides the operational platform. RestoreAssist provides the client interface. The Structure → Systems → Scale education pathway exists. The international movement has already demonstrated that the model works.

The conditions in ANZ today are the conditions that existed in the US when the independence movement began to gain real traction. The operators who moved early in the US built the strongest independent businesses in the market. The same window is open in ANZ.

It will not stay open indefinitely.

## The Connection to the Global Movement

The conviction behind NRPG is not new.

The same argument — that the restoration contractor is a professional who works for the property owner, not the program — has been made, proven, and built into a movement in other markets. The intellectual framework exists. The business models have been stress-tested. The community infrastructure that accelerates individual operator development has been refined over years of real-world application.

NRPG is the ANZ expression of that global reality.

Not a franchise. Not a copy. An authentic, locally-built movement that is informed by international proof and adapted to the specific dynamics of the Australian and New Zealand market — the insurer relationships, the regulatory environment, the managed repair program structures, and the particular character of the ANZ restoration practitioner.

The global movement got there first. ANZ has the advantage of learning from what they built — and building faster because of it.
