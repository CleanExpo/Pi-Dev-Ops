## Email 2 — Your First Action

**Subject:** One thing. This week. Non-negotiable.

**Send timing:** Day 3

---

There's a lot you could do. There's one thing you should do first.

Calculate your real managed repair margin.

Not revenue. Not what you think your margin is. Your actual net margin on panel jobs — after admin time, variation disputes, portal compliance, payment delays, and the cost of scope arguments that don't go your way.

Most restorers who do this calculation properly get a number between 8% and 14%. The ones who are brutally honest often find themselves below 10%.

That number matters because everything starts there. It's the number that tells you how urgent the transition is. It's the number you'll refer back to in six months when you're running independent jobs at 28–35% margin. It's the number that makes the NRPG path concrete, not abstract.

Here's how to do it properly:

Take your last twelve months of panel jobs. Pull the gross revenue. Now subtract: materials at real cost, labour at award rates (not cheap), vehicles, insurance, portal time (put an hourly rate on it — your hourly rate), variation disputes lost, payment-delay cost (you're financing these jobs — that has a cost), and any compliance levies the panel takes off the top.

Whatever's left — that's your real margin.

Write that number down. Screenshot it. Keep it. That's your "why."

One of the NRPG members in the community — a 7-person operation in SEQ — did this calculation in his second month on panels and got 9.4%. He's now running 31% on direct and insurance-broker-referred work. The calculation was the turning point.

Your business cannot improve if you don't know what's actually happening inside it.

**Action:** Do the margin calculation this week. Use the NRPG Margin Audit Template in your member resources. It takes 45 minutes. The number will change how you think about everything.

## Email 3 — The Framework

**Subject:** Structure → Systems → Scale. Here's what it actually means.

**Send timing:** Day 7

---

Every NRPG member works through the same three-phase framework. Not because it's the only way — because after examining independent ANZ restoration businesses of every size, these three phases appear in every successful transition. In sequence. Always.

**Phase 1: Structure**

Structure comes before everything else. This is the unglamorous phase — the one most restorers skip, which is exactly why they get stuck later.

Structure means: legal and commercial foundation (contracts, scoping language, T&Cs that hold up), pricing architecture (a rate card you built, not one assigned to you), positioning (a clear statement of who you serve and why they should choose you over a panel contractor), and the first direct relationships — insurers, brokers, strata managers, facility managers — that don't come through a managed repair portal.

You cannot systematise or scale a structurally weak business. You'll just build a machine that breaks faster.

Timeline: 60–90 days. This is not optional. Do not skip to Systems.

**Phase 2: Systems**

Systems is where the business stops being founder-dependent. This is scoping systems, job management workflows, invoicing and variation processes, technician briefing standards, quality-check protocols. Documented. Repeatable. Teachable to someone you haven't hired yet.

Systems means the business runs at the same quality whether you're on-site or not. It's also the phase where you start to see margin improvement — not from charging more yet, but from removing the leakage that comes from inconsistency.

Timeline: 90–180 days.

**Phase 3: Scale**

Scale is earned. Once Structure and Systems are in place, growth isn't risk — it's multiplication. New markets, new service lines, commercial work, national accounts. Scale on a weak structure breaks things. Scale on a documented system multiplies margin.

This is also where the Restopreneur™ distinction becomes real — a restoration business that operates as an enterprise, not a job.

Most NRPG members spend 12–18 months across all three phases. Some do it in 9. None do it properly in less than 6.

Where are you? There's a self-assessment in your member portal that maps your current state to the framework. Take it this week — it determines which resources you need first.

**Action:** Complete the Structure → Systems → Scale self-assessment in the NRPG portal. It takes 10 minutes and produces a personalised resource roadmap.

Do not take panel work as a short-term bridge unless you have a documented exit plan with a specific date. The panel trap takes less than six months to close around you.
