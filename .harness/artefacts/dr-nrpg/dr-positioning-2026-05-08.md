# Disaster Recovery (DR) — Positioning Document

---

## One-Sentence Position

DR is the operational platform built specifically for ANZ restoration businesses that have outgrown the owner's memory and need systems, visibility, and control to scale.

---

## The Problem

Most restoration businesses in ANZ are built on the owner. The owner knows where every job is. The owner knows which tech is on what site. The owner knows which documents are outstanding, which insurance contacts to call, which jobs are about to breach SLA.

That is not a business. That is a person with a job they cannot leave.

When the business lives in one person's head, nothing scales. Hiring becomes a liability. Growth creates chaos. The operator who built a $2M business by knowing everything cannot build a $5M business the same way. The knowledge that made them successful becomes the ceiling that stops them.

The specific failure points are consistent across the industry:

- **No job visibility** — status lives in text threads, verbal updates, and memory
- **No compliance architecture** — documentation is reactive, assembled under pressure at claim close
- **No operational continuity** — if the owner steps back, jobs fall through the cracks
- **No data** — decisions are made on gut feel because no system is capturing performance

The result: good operators working too hard, growing too slowly, burning out, and being systematically undervalued in an ecosystem designed to keep them dependent.

---

## Three Product Pillars

### 1. Operations Platform
End-to-end job management built for restoration workflows — not adapted from construction or field service software. Job creation, technician dispatch, site documentation, progress tracking, SLA monitoring, and close-out in a single system. The business runs whether the owner is on-site or not.

### 2. Practitioner Network
DR connects to NRPG — the National Restoration Practitioners Group — giving platform users access to the ANZ restoration community, peer benchmarking, shared knowledge, and the collective intelligence of independent operators. Technology and community are not separate. They reinforce each other.

### 3. Compliance Integration
Insurance restoration operates inside a compliance framework that most operators manage manually and badly. DR builds compliance into the workflow — moisture logs, photo documentation, scope sign-off, and close-out packs generated from the job record, not assembled from scattered files at the end. Audit-ready, always.

## Target Customer

**The established ANZ restoration business that has outgrown spreadsheets.**

- 5–25 staff
- $1.5M–$8M revenue
- Running on a mix of spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and owner oversight
- Has experienced at least one painful job where something fell through because there was no system
- Owner is working in the business more than on it
- Knows they need systems — has not yet found one built for this industry

This is not a startup needing their first process. This is a capable operator whose current tools cannot keep up with their ambition.

---

## Platform Vision: The Complete ANZ Restoration Business Operating System

DR does not exist in isolation. It is the operational core of a three-part ecosystem:

**DR** — the job and business management platform  
**NRPG** — the practitioner community, education framework, and independence movement  
**RestoreAssist** — client-facing and insurance-interface tooling

Together, these three form the complete ANZ restoration business operating system. An operator running on DR, connected to NRPG, and using RestoreAssist has:

- A system that runs their operations
- A community that accelerates their growth
- Tools that strengthen their position with insurers and property owners

No other combination in ANZ delivers this. The international equivalents in the US market have demonstrated that when technology and community align, independent restorers stop competing on price and start competing on capability.

---

## DR and the Managed Repair Independence Movement

The ANZ managed repair ecosystem extracts margin from restoration contractors. Preferred supplier programs, managed repair networks, and insurer-directed work relationships structurally favour the insurer and the managed repair organisation — not the contractor doing the work.

DR is built on the belief that the contractor's leverage is operational excellence. An operator who can prove job quality, demonstrate compliance, document outcomes, and show performance data does not need to accept managed repair terms as the default.

DR gives operators the infrastructure to build that proof. Visibility. Documentation. Performance records. The ability to demonstrate — not just claim — professional capability.

This connects directly to what NRPG represents as a movement: the ANZ restorer who works for the property owner, not the program. DR is the technology layer that makes that position commercially viable, not just philosophically desirable.

The platform does not tell operators to leave managed repair programs. It gives them the capability to not need them.
