## Profile 1 — PRIMARY: The "Ready to Break Free" Restorer

### Firmographics
- **Industry:** Insurance repair / restoration (water, fire, mould, contents)
- **Staff:** 3–10 (owner-operator or first management hire)
- **Revenue:** $800K–$2.5M annual
- **Geography:** QLD, NSW, VIC — suburban and regional markets
- **Tenure:** 5–15 years in business, 2–8 years on managed repair panels
- **Tech:** BPMS / Xactimate / some proprietary insurer portal; no real business systems of their own

### Role profile
| Role | Title | KPIs they're measured on |
|---|---|---|
| Decision maker | Owner / Director | Net margin, crew utilisation, job completion rate |
| Champion | (themselves) | Getting off the hamster wheel |
| Blocker | Business partner, spouse, accountant | "Don't rock the boat — the panel keeps the lights on" |

### Pain hierarchy
| # | Pain | Frequency | Severity | Awareness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Adjuster overrides scope — margin disappears on disputed line items | Weekly | $200–$800 per job | Actively aware, bitterly aware |
| 2 | Panel pricing hasn't moved in 3–5 years; their costs have | Daily | 8–15% margin erosion | Aware |
| 3 | No pricing power — cannot pass on wage or material cost increases | Monthly | Cumulative, compounding | Aware |
| 4 | Administrative overhead from insurer portals eats 6–10 hrs/week | Daily | $300–600 in labour cost | Latent |
| 5 | No identity outside the panel — no own brand, no direct customer relationships | Latent | Strategic | Latent |

### Trigger events (move from "frustrated" to "looking for a way out")
- A single job where the adjuster overrides the scope strips $1,200+ in margin — the moment they do the maths on how often this happens
- A peer (same market, similar size) gets off a panel and talks about what changed — income, autonomy, time
- Panel sends revised pricing terms — lower rates, higher compliance obligations
- Owner's accountant shows year-end P&L and net margin is under 12%
- An insurer's 90-day payment cycle creates a cashflow crisis

### The conversation already happening in their head
*"I built this business. I know how to restore a property. But I'm working for a rate someone else set three years ago and I can't even argue a variation without a two-week fight. I've got six techs, two vans, fifteen years of experience — and I'm taking home less per job than when I started. Something has to change. I just don't know what the other side looks like."*

### What they're afraid of
- Losing the volume. The panel is predictable. Walking away from it feels like walking off a cliff.
- Not knowing how to find private work — no sales skills, no marketing, no direct insurer or broker relationships
- Looking stupid to staff if the transition fails
- The adjuster finding out they're looking to leave and cutting their allocation

### What they want from NRPG
- Proof it works — case studies from people their size who got off panels without going broke
- A clear path. Not theory — a step-by-step transition framework
- Peers who understand the ANZ managed repair context (not US advice applied badly)
- Negotiation tactics for adjuster disputes RIGHT NOW, before the full transition
- Legitimacy: being part of a professional body that makes them look like more than a panel contractor

### What message converts them
**"You're not underpaid because you're not good enough. You're underpaid because you're playing by someone else's rules. NRPG is for restorers who've decided to stop."**

Proof format: revenue numbers (not percentages), real ANZ business names where possible, timeline to first independent job.

## Profile 2 — SECONDARY: The Established Independent

### Firmographics
- **Industry:** Restoration — typically broader scope (water, fire, mould + contents or specialty)
- **Staff:** 10–20 (has management layer — ops manager or project managers)
- **Revenue:** $2M–$6M annual
- **Geography:** Capital cities and major regionals, NSW/VIC/QLD/WA
- **Tenure:** Off panels for 2–7 years; built independence intuitively

### Role profile
| Role | Title | KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| Decision maker | Owner / MD | EBITDA, staff retention, repeat client rate |
| Champion | Ops Manager | Systems, scalability |
| User | Project Managers | Consistent process, clear scope |

### Pain hierarchy
| # | Pain | Frequency | Severity | Awareness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Business runs on the founder's relationships and instincts — not transferable systems | Daily | Ceiling on growth, key-person risk | Aware |
| 2 | Pricing is inconsistent — different PMs quote differently, margin varies wildly | Weekly | 5–10% margin variance per job | Aware |
| 3 | No peer group — operating in isolation without benchmarks or frameworks | Monthly | Strategic drift | Latent |
| 4 | Staff don't know "the NRPG way" — culture is tribal, not documented | Monthly | Recruitment and retention risk | Latent |
| 5 | No professional validation for what they've built — feels informal | Quarterly | Business valuation, client confidence | Latent |

### Trigger events
- Tries to hire a second project manager and realises there are no systems to hand over
- Considers selling the business and gets asked "what are your documented processes?"
- Loses a key commercial client because a competitor looked more professional/certified
- Reads a US restoration industry article and recognises what they've built — but also what's missing

### The conversation already happening in their head
*"I figured out how to run this thing. It works. But it only works because I'm here. If I'm not here for two weeks, things slip. I want to build something that doesn't need me in every decision. I've read the E-Myth. I know what I need to do. I just need the framework, the vocabulary, and some people who've done it at this scale in Australia."*

### What they're afraid of
- Systematising the wrong things and losing the client relationships that are the actual asset
- Wasting time in a community full of people at a lower level — wanting peers, not students
- Adopting US frameworks that don't work in the ANZ insurance/restoration context
- Being "sold to" when they join — wanting community, not a funnel

### What they want from NRPG
- Peer-level community with operators their size and bigger
- Frameworks for the next phase: delegation, systems, valuation
- Benchmarking data — margin, revenue per tech, close rates on commercial work
- Professional credential that reinforces their market position
- A vehicle for sharing what they've figured out (status + contribution)

### What message converts them
**"You built it on instinct. NRPG gives you the framework to make it run without you. Join the operators who are doing the same thing — and the ones who already have."**

Proof format: framework previews, member profiles at their revenue level, community activity/quality signal.

## Profile 3 — TERTIARY: The New Entrant

### Firmographics
- **Industry:** Restoration (typically starting in water damage or general remediation)
- **Staff:** 0–2 (sole operator or owner + one tech)
- **Revenue:** $0–$400K (pre-revenue to early traction)
- **Geography:** Any ANZ market
- **Tenure:** 0–2 years in business; often coming from a trade background (plumbing, carpentry, remediation tech)

### Role profile
| Role | Title | KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| Decision maker | Owner / Operator | First paying clients, first profitable month |
| Champion | (themselves) | Not making the mistakes they've heard about |
| Blocker | Fear of complexity, lack of capital | "I can't afford to get this wrong" |

### Pain hierarchy
| # | Pain | Frequency | Severity | Awareness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Don't know what they don't know — no model for running a restoration business | Daily | Risk of fatal early mistakes | Latent |
| 2 | Managed repair panels look like the only way to get work quickly | Weekly | Trap risk | Latent |
| 3 | No pricing knowledge — don't know what to charge or how to justify it | Every quote | Revenue at risk | Aware |
| 4 | No professional network — operating alone | Daily | Isolation, slow learning | Latent |
| 5 | No credentialing or certification path — hard to win commercial work | Monthly | Revenue ceiling | Aware |

### Trigger events
- Gets offered a panel position and something feels off — asks in a forum, gets told "avoid if you can"
- Hears a podcast or reads content that explains the managed repair trap
- Tries to price a job and has no reference point — googles and finds nothing useful for ANZ
- Meets an independent restorer who is visibly running a better business

### The conversation already happening in their head
*"I know the technical side. I've been doing this for years as a tech. I can run a job. But I don't know how to run a business. And I'm hearing that if I sign up for these insurance panels, I'm locking myself into a bad deal for years. I want to build this right from the start. I need someone to show me the path — what to set up, what to avoid, what order to do things in."*

### What they're afraid of
- Starting with the panels because they seem safe and getting stuck — the "boiling frog" trap
- Spending money on training or community that doesn't give them practical return
- Being too green to contribute — impostor syndrome in a community of established operators
- Getting advice from people who don't understand the ANZ market

### What they want from NRPG
- A clear starting framework — what to set up in year one (legal, pricing, positioning, first clients)
- Permission structure: a way to get credentialed without years of experience first
- Access to more experienced members willing to mentor
- A community where it's safe to ask basic questions
- Content that directly addresses the managed repair trap before they fall in

### What message converts them
**"Start independent. The panel looks like the easy on-ramp — it isn't. NRPG shows you how to build it right from day one."**

Proof format: specific first-year actions, member stories from people who started with NRPG before panels, entry-level resource previews.
