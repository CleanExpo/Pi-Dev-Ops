## NRPG EXISTS BECAUSE...

The ANZ restoration industry has a structural problem. It is designed — deliberately or otherwise — to keep the contractor dependent. Managed repair programs, preferred supplier relationships, insurer-directed work: these arrangements have the same thing in common. They extract margin from the person doing the work, and they condition the contractor to compete on compliance rather than capability.

The result is an industry full of technically skilled operators running businesses that can't scale, can't exit, and can't survive a bad month with the program. The knowledge is there. The craft is there. The systems, the community, and the framework to build past it are not — or they weren't.

NRPG exists because the independence movement that transformed restoration businesses in other markets has not yet arrived in ANZ at scale. Because the operators who are ready to build differently deserve access to the peer group, the education, and the technology that makes it possible. Because working for the property owner — not the program — is not just a philosophy. It is a business model. And it works. We are building the infrastructure to prove it in ANZ.

## WHAT YOU GET AS A MEMBER

**1. The peer group**
Direct access to a community of ANZ restoration practitioners who are building the same way you want to build. Peer forums, case studies, working groups, and operators who will tell you exactly what worked and what didn't — without the filter of a trade association or a program with a commercial interest in the answer.

**2. The Restopreneur™ Education Framework: Structure → Systems → Scale**
A practical, sequenced curriculum built specifically for restoration business owners. Not generic small business advice. Not motivational content. A framework that starts with the structural foundations your business needs, builds the operational systems that let it run without you, and then teaches you how to scale it without recreating the chaos. Workshops, deep-dives, and direct application to your business.

**3. The technology stack**
NRPG members get access to DR — the operational platform built for ANZ restoration — and RestoreAssist, the client-facing interface that positions you as a professional service provider. These are not discounted third-party tools. They are platforms built for this community, by this community, to do exactly one job: make the independent operator model commercially viable, not just philosophically desirable.

**4. The commercial positioning shift**
This is the hardest one to quantify and the most important. NRPG gives you the framework, the language, and the documented capability to change your relationship with insurers, assessors, and property owners. The operator who can demonstrate performance, document outcomes, and articulate value is not competing with the cheapest option. They are in a different conversation entirely. NRPG builds the capability that changes the conversation.

## THE COMMUNITY

### Who else is in NRPG

NRPG members are established ANZ restoration operators who are past the startup phase and ready to build differently.

They run businesses with 3–20 staff. They have managed repair relationships they're actively trying to reduce or renegotiate. They take their own professional development seriously — they read, they listen, they invest in getting better. They are tired of competing on price with operators who are undercutting on margin they don't understand.

They are not all the same size. They are not all at the same stage. But they share a single orientation: they believe the restoration contractor is a professional who deserves a business model that reflects that, and they are willing to do the work to build one.

This is the peer group that accelerates your development faster than any other input. Not because they have all the answers — but because they are asking the same questions, in the same market, at the same time.

## THIS IS NOT FOR EVERYONE

**NRPG is not the right community for every restoration contractor.**

If you are satisfied with your managed repair program and have no interest in building past it — this is not for you. There is no judgment in that. It is just not what NRPG is about.

If you are looking for a trade association that advocates on your behalf without asking you to change your business model — this is not that.

If you are not willing to invest in your own development — in the systems, the education, and the work of building a business that doesn't run on your personal effort alone — NRPG will not help you. The framework only works if you use it.

If your goal is to extract maximum short-term revenue from the cheapest-cost-per-job model, and professional positioning is not something you value — you will not get value from this community.

NRPG is a community of practitioners who have made a specific choice: to build businesses that are structurally sound, operationally capable, and commercially independent. That choice requires work. It requires investment. And it requires the willingness to operate differently to how most of the industry currently operates.

If that is the choice you are ready to make — or the choice you are already making in isolation and want to make with a community behind you — then NRPG is exactly where you belong.
