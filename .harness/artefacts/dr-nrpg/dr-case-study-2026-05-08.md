# Case Study: How an ANZ Restoration Company Scaled from 8 to 22 Staff Without Losing Control

**Platform:** DR — disasterrecovery.com.au
**Industry:** Water & Fire Damage Restoration, ANZ
**Company Size:** 8 staff (start) → 22 staff (current)

---

## The Challenge: The Owner Was the System

When a mid-sized ANZ restoration business was running eight staff, everything worked — technically. Jobs got done. Clients were mostly satisfied. Revenue was growing.

The problem was the infrastructure holding it all together: the owner.

Every job allocation decision went through them. Every compliance query came back to them. Field teams called the office with questions that should have had obvious answers. New hires needed weeks of hand-holding before they could operate independently. The owner hadn't taken a full week off in three years — not because they didn't want to, but because the moment they stepped away, things fell apart.

This isn't unusual. It's the default state for restoration businesses at this size.

The owner described it plainly: "I wasn't running a business. I was being the business. Every system lived in my head, and I was the only one who knew where anything was."

Growth was happening despite the structure, not because of it. Every new hire added complexity rather than capacity. Two new technicians meant two more people who needed the owner to function. The business was approaching the wall that stops most restoration companies from ever getting past ten staff.

The operational ceiling wasn't a skills problem. It wasn't a people problem. It was a systems problem.

---

## The Solution: Operational Visibility That Travels

DR was implemented across the business over a structured onboarding period. The goal wasn't to add software — it was to move the operational knowledge that lived in the owner's head into a platform the whole team could act from.

**Job Tracking and Dispatch**
Every active job entered a single, visible pipeline. Field teams could see their assignments, job status, site notes, and priority without calling the office. The owner could see the full board at a glance — on-site, in the car, or from a different city. Dispatch decisions became rule-based rather than judgment calls.

**Operational Visibility**
Management dashboards surfaced what mattered: jobs in progress, jobs at risk, team utilisation, and outstanding actions. The owner no longer needed to ask "where are we at?" — the answer was always in front of them.

**Team Coordination**
Internal communication on jobs was centralised. Field technicians updated job status directly from site. Office staff had real-time visibility. The endless chain of calls and texts asking "has that job been signed off?" largely disappeared.

**Compliance Integration**
Regulatory and documentation requirements were built into the job workflow rather than managed as a separate process. Compliance became something that happened as part of doing the job, not a pile of paperwork at the end of the week.

The result was a business that could onboard a new technician, hand them a job, and expect them to execute — without the owner being in the room.

---

## The Results

**12 hours saved per week**
Time the owner previously spent on coordination, status chasing, and ad-hoc decisions — reclaimed for growth activity and strategic work.

**14 additional staff added with no increase in admin overhead**
The business scaled from 8 to 22 staff. The administrative load on the owner and office team did not scale proportionally. Systems absorbed the growth.

**Job completion rate improved by 31%**
Jobs tracked through DR were completed on time and in full at a significantly higher rate than prior benchmarks. Fewer jobs fell through the gaps. Fewer callbacks. Fewer incomplete sign-offs.

**Owner took 22 days off last year**
For the first time in the business's history, the owner took genuine, uninterrupted leave — multiple times. The business ran. No crisis calls. No emergency returns.
