**Unite-Group Portfolio | ANZ Market**

## Category Position

CARSI is the compliance delivery platform for trade and restoration businesses in Australia and New Zealand — structured, auditable, and built for the operational realities of the field.

---

## The Core Problem

Compliance in trade-heavy industries is fragmented by design — and broken in practice.

A restoration business managing 20 active jobs is simultaneously navigating WHS obligations, subcontractor certifications, insurance documentation, regulatory reporting, and client-facing audit trails. A construction firm crossing the 10-staff threshold suddenly faces a compliance surface they have no system to manage.

The result is the same across industries: compliance lives in folders, inboxes, spreadsheets, and the memory of whoever handles it. Audits expose gaps. Gaps produce liability. Liability costs more than any system would have.

**Compliance fragmentation is not a documentation problem. It is a delivery problem.** The paperwork exists. The regulations are known. The failure point is the workflow — the gap between obligation and evidence.

CARSI closes that gap.

---

## Positioning Pillars

### Pillar 1: Structured Delivery
**"Compliance doesn't happen. It's delivered."**

CARSI treats compliance as a workflow, not an administrative category. Every obligation — certification, inspection, regulatory report, audit document — exists inside a structured delivery pipeline. Nothing is ad hoc. Nothing is missed because someone forgot to check a folder.

Compliance that runs itself means the system carries the obligation forward, not the person.

### Pillar 2: Audit Readiness at All Times
**"You're always one request away from an audit. CARSI makes that acceptable."**

The trigger for most compliance investment is an audit that went poorly. CARSI is built for the business that never wants to be in that position again — and for the business that wants to foreclose that risk entirely.

Every record, every certification, every compliance event is timestamped, searchable, and presentable. Not on the night before the audit. Always.

### Pillar 3: Operational Integration
**"Compliance that sits outside operations gets ignored. CARSI lives inside it."**

Standalone compliance tools fail because they require a separate habit. CARSI integrates into existing operational workflows — connecting to field management, job tracking, and reporting systems. For RestoreAssist users, this integration is native.

When compliance is embedded in how the business runs, adoption is not a change management problem.

## Target Audience

### Who Buys CARSI

**Primary buyer:** Operations manager, compliance manager, or owner-operator in a trade or restoration business with 10–150 staff. The buyer is accountable for compliance outcomes but does not have the administrative infrastructure of a large enterprise. They are resourceful, practical, and deeply aware of what happens when compliance breaks down.

**Secondary buyer:** Group operations leads at multi-site businesses (restoration networks, construction groups, facilities management firms) who need compliance consistency across locations they cannot personally supervise.

### Trigger Events

These are the moments that convert interest into purchase:

1. **Audit failure or near-miss** — A regulatory audit exposed gaps, or a client audit created a serious conversation. The business cannot afford to repeat it.
2. **Regulatory change** — New WHS legislation, updated building codes, or insurance requirements have created obligations the business does not yet have a system to meet.
3. **Scaling past 10 staff** — The informal systems that worked at eight people do not work at fifteen. Certifications are expiring unnoticed. Subcontractor documentation is inconsistent. The owner is no longer close enough to the detail.
4. **Acquisition or due diligence** — A business being acquired or preparing for sale needs a clean, demonstrable compliance record. The absence of one reduces value or kills the deal.

## What CARSI Is Not

- CARSI is not a document storage platform. Documents are outputs of compliance workflows, not the compliance system itself.
- CARSI is not a generic project management tool with a compliance template.
- CARSI is not built for enterprise legal and governance teams. It is built for operational businesses that need compliance to run without a dedicated compliance department.
- CARSI is not a consultancy. It does not produce compliance advice. It delivers compliance execution.
- CARSI is not a one-size-fits-all SaaS product with a self-serve trial designed for the SMB segment. It is an enterprise-grade delivery platform sold to businesses that take compliance seriously.

## CARSI Within the Unite-Group Portfolio

### Connection to RestoreAssist

RestoreAssist is Unite-Group's job and project management platform for the restoration industry. It manages the operational layer — job tracking, scoping, subcontractor coordination, client communication.

CARSI sits alongside RestoreAssist as the compliance layer for the same operators. A restoration business running RestoreAssist already has job data, subcontractor records, and site documentation flowing through a system. CARSI connects to that data and converts it into a structured compliance record — certifications, safety documentation, regulatory reporting — without requiring a separate administrative effort.

For restoration businesses, this is the value of the Unite-Group platform stack: operational management and compliance delivery from the same portfolio, built to connect.

### Connection to NRPG

NRPG (National Restoration Purchasing Group) is Unite-Group's network for restoration operators — commercial relationships, preferred suppliers, and industry alignment. NRPG members operate in a compliance-intensive environment: insurance work, insurer audits, contractor qualification panels, and WHS obligations.

CARSI is the natural compliance infrastructure for NRPG network members. The combination of NRPG's commercial network and CARSI's compliance delivery creates a defensible ecosystem: operators who are commercially aligned through NRPG and compliance-aligned through CARSI are deeply integrated into the Unite-Group platform and difficult to displace.

## The Acquisition Case

### Why Compliance Platforms Command Premium Multiples

Compliance software occupies a structurally advantaged position in any acquisition analysis:

- **Non-discretionary spend.** Compliance is not a productivity tool. It is a risk management obligation. Businesses do not cancel compliance software in a downturn.
- **High switching cost.** Compliance records, audit histories, and workflow configurations are not portable. Once a business's compliance history is inside CARSI, migration is a business risk, not an IT task.
- **Regulatory tailwinds.** ANZ regulatory requirements are expanding, not contracting. Each new obligation is a retention driver.
- **Workflow embeddedness.** A compliance platform integrated into operational workflows (RestoreAssist, field management systems) is not a standalone SaaS product. It is infrastructure. Infrastructure multiples exceed feature software multiples.

### Why CARSI Fits

CARSI is not a point solution. It is a compliance delivery platform with:

- Live implementations and a growing customer base in compliance-intensive industries
- Native integration with RestoreAssist and the NRPG ecosystem (defended distribution)
- An ANZ-specific regulatory framework that creates a moat against generic international competitors
- Revenue that is contractual, recurring, and tied to operational dependency rather than discretionary use

For a strategic acquirer in the insurance, construction technology, or risk management space, CARSI is not a feature to build — it is a position to acquire. The compliance delivery category in ANZ trade industries is early. The platform that owns workflow integration at this stage is difficult to displace at any stage.
