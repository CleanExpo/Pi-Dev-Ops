## Use Case 1: Restoration Contractor Pre-Audit Preparation

### The Problem
A 15-person restoration company receives notice of a WHS audit in 48 hours. Historically, preparing for an audit like this has meant two weeks of scrambling: pulling paper records, chasing certificates of currency, reconciling incident logs, and manually verifying that every worker's inductions and licences are current. The operations manager estimates 40+ hours of admin time — time the business doesn't have, and accuracy it can't guarantee.

### The CARSI Approach
CARSI holds the company's entire compliance record in a single, audit-ready system. The moment audit notice is received, the operations manager generates a compliance snapshot: all SWMS documents, worker inductions, subcontractor insurances, incident reports, and training certifications — current, verified, and timestamped. Gaps are flagged automatically. Expired documents trigger immediate alerts with resolution workflows. The auditor-ready export package is formatted to WHS regulatory standards.

### The Outcome
Audit preparation is completed in under four hours. Every document is traceable to source. No surprises, no scrambling. The company enters the audit with confidence rather than anxiety.

### Key Metric
**Preparation time: 48 hours → 4 hours. Manual admin eliminated: ~38 hours per audit cycle.**

## Use Case 2: Construction Site Compliance Across Multiple Active Sites

### The Problem
A mid-tier construction company is running three active sites simultaneously — a commercial fit-out in Sydney, a residential development in Brisbane, and an industrial warehouse in Melbourne. Each site has different subcontractors, different SWMS requirements, and different incident reporting obligations. The compliance manager is managing this across spreadsheets, email threads, and site-specific paper files. Documents fall through the cracks. Inductions expire unnoticed. An incident at the Melbourne site is logged three days late.

### The CARSI Approach
CARSI gives the compliance manager a single dashboard across all three sites. Contractor inductions are issued, tracked, and renewed digitally — workers cannot access a site until their induction is confirmed complete. SWMS documents are version-controlled, site-specific, and require worker sign-off before any high-risk work begins. Incident reports are lodged in real time via mobile, automatically routed to the compliance manager and relevant site supervisor, and formatted for regulatory submission.

### The Outcome
Every site operates to the same compliance standard. The compliance manager has full visibility across all three locations from one screen. Incident reporting lag drops from days to minutes. Subcontractor non-compliance is caught before it becomes a liability.

### Key Metric
**Incident reporting time: 72 hours → under 30 minutes. Induction compliance rate across all sites: 100%.**

## Use Case 3: Facilities Management — 50-Property Portfolio

### The Problem
A facilities management company overseeing 50 commercial properties is drowning in compliance obligations. Preventive maintenance schedules must be documented and verified. Tradespeople carrying out certifiable work (electrical, plumbing, fire safety) must hold current licences and insurances. Regulatory reporting — fire safety statements, essential services records, lift certifications — must be submitted on time to building owners and councils. The current process involves a combination of spreadsheets, calendar reminders, and manual file-chasing. Missed deadlines have already resulted in two non-compliance notices in the past year.

### The CARSI Approach
CARSI maps every property against its compliance obligations: maintenance schedules, certification expiry dates, and regulatory reporting deadlines. Preventive maintenance records are logged against each property and automatically reconciled against schedule. Tradesperson credentials are held in CARSI and verified before work orders are assigned — no unlicensed contractor can be dispatched. Regulatory reports are generated from live data and scheduled for submission ahead of deadlines, with escalation alerts if sign-off is delayed.

### The Outcome
The compliance manager has a live view of the entire portfolio's compliance status at any moment. No deadline is missed. No unqualified tradesperson slips through. Reporting that previously took three days per cycle now takes under two hours.

### Key Metric
**Regulatory non-compliance notices: 2 per year → 0. Reporting cycle time reduced by 85%.**

## Use Case 4: New Regulatory Requirement — ISO Standard Update

### The Problem
A revised ISO standard affecting work health and safety management systems is gazetted with a 90-day transition period. A national trade services business operating across four states has 23 active clients, each with documentation referencing the superseded standard. Updating every procedure, policy, and compliance record manually would take weeks, require specialist review, and carry significant risk of version inconsistency — some documents updated, others not.

### The CARSI Approach
When the regulatory change is confirmed, CARSI's compliance team issues a system-wide update. The new standard is mapped against all affected document types in the platform. Clients receive an automated impact assessment: which documents are affected, what changes are required, and the deadline for transition. Document templates are updated centrally. Clients can review, approve, and publish updated documentation through CARSI — with full version control, sign-off records, and audit trails preserved throughout.

### The Outcome
All 23 client accounts are transitioned to the new standard within the 90-day window. No client is left on outdated documentation. Every change is traceable: who approved it, when, and what the previous version stated. The business meets its regulatory obligation with zero manual document-hunting.

### Key Metric
**Client transition time: estimated 6–8 weeks manual effort → completed across all accounts in under 2 weeks. Version inconsistency risk: eliminated.**
