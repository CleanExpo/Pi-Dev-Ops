---

Three signs compliance has the wrong relationship with your business:

**1. You only know your compliance status when someone asks.**
Not because you're disorganised. Because the data lives in six places and reconciling it takes hours. Compliance visibility should be continuous — not a project you do before an audit.

**2. Your compliance manager is the single point of failure.**
If one person holds the knowledge, the records, and the process — you don't have a compliance system. You have a compliance dependency. That's a different problem.

**3. Preparing for audits takes longer than delivering the project.**
Audit preparation is retrieval, not creation. If it takes days or weeks, the underlying record system isn't working. Audit-ready should be the permanent state.

These aren't signs of a failing business. They're signs of a compliance function that was built for a smaller operation and hasn't been redesigned since.

## Post 8: What Does Good Compliance Actually Look Like in 2026?

**Hook:** Good compliance in 2026 doesn't look like a filing cabinet. It doesn't look like a spreadsheet either.

---

The compliance managers we most respect don't talk about compliance the way most people in the industry do.

They don't describe it as a burden or a box to tick. They describe it as operational clarity.

Here's what good compliance actually looks like in 2026:

**It's live, not retrospective.**
Records are captured at the point of work. Not reconstructed before an audit. You know your compliance status today — not after three hours of reconciliation.

**It's distributed, not dependent.**
Every site supervisor, every subcontractor, every worker has access to the information they need to do their job compliantly. It's not held by one person in one system on one laptop.

**It's proportionate, not performative.**
Good compliance matches documentation rigour to actual risk. It doesn't create paperwork for paperwork's sake. It captures what matters and ignores what doesn't.

**It's verifiable, not assumed.**
Every certification, every sign-off, every incident report has a trail. Not because someone might check — but because that's what it means to actually know your compliance status.
