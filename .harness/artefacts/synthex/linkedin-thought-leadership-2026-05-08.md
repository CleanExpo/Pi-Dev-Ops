**Hook:**
When someone asks ChatGPT "best electrical supplier in Brisbane" — your business either gets named, or it doesn't. That's AEO.

**Body:**
Answer Engine Optimisation. You haven't heard of it yet, but your competitors will be talking about it in 12 months.

SEO was about ranking in Google's blue links. AEO is about being the answer AI gives when someone asks a question directly.

The shift is real. Roughly 30% of searches that used to end on Google now end inside ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Gemini. The user types a question, the AI synthesises an answer from across the web, and either your business gets cited — or it doesn't.

The businesses getting cited aren't necessarily the biggest. They're the most structured. AI engines favour:
- Clear, consistent content across multiple platforms
- Defined expertise signals (what category do you own?)
- Entity authority (do multiple sources confirm who you are?)

For ANZ trade businesses, this matters now because the purchasing behaviour of your buyers is changing. The tradie doing a supplier comparison on their phone between jobs isn't scrolling through ten websites. They're asking an AI.

You either show up in the answer, or you lose the consideration entirely.

## Post 3: The 34% Open Rate Story

**Hook:**
18% to 34% email open rate. Same list. Same business. Here's exactly what changed.

**Body:**
A trade supplies business in regional Queensland. 12 employees. One person handling marketing on the side of their actual job. Email open rates sitting at 18% — not bad, not good.

They made three changes. Ninety days later: 34%.

**Change 1: Subject lines written by AI trained on the owner's voice.**

Not generic "weekly update" subject lines. Not clickbait. Subject lines that sounded like the owner writing to a customer — specific, direct, and clearly from a real person. Their list had been conditioned to expect corporate noise. When the emails started sounding human again, opens went up.

**Change 2: Send time based on their actual data, not best-practice blogs.**

Industry advice said Thursday at 11am. Their audience data said Tuesday at 7:10am — before the first job started. That single change moved the needle by 8 points alone.

**Change 3: Cadence consistency.**

They went from "when I remember" to a fixed weekly schedule. Consistent senders get unconsciously whitelisted by subscribers. After six weeks of reliability, opens stopped declining between sends.

None of these changes required a new list, a rebrand, or a big campaign budget.

They required a system. And a system requires the right tools.

This isn't a one-business anomaly. It's a pattern we see across trade SMBs: the fundamentals, done consistently with AI doing the heavy lifting, compound faster than most businesses expect.

The businesses using AI content effectively aren't typing better prompts. They've trained the system on who they actually are.

For trade businesses in particular, authentic voice is a competitive advantage. Your buyers are sharp on authenticity. When your content sounds like a real person who knows the industry, trust transfers faster.

## Post 7: Content Calendar Automation

**Hook:**
Most content calendars die by week three. Here's why — and what the fix actually looks like.

**Body:**
The content calendar problem isn't discipline. It's design.

A spreadsheet-based calendar asks you to do three jobs at once: decide what to create, create it, and schedule it. When you're running a trade business, that's three jobs that consistently lose to more urgent work. The calendar goes cold. You feel guilty. You start again next month.

AI-driven content calendars are designed differently. The distinction matters:

**Traditional calendar:**
You decide → you create → you schedule → calendar dies when you stop

**AI-driven calendar:**
You set direction → AI generates options aligned to your voice → you approve or edit → AI schedules and adapts based on performance

The second model is sustainable because the creation step — the hardest and most time-consuming — is handled. You're a director, not a writer.

For ANZ trade businesses, the most effective calendar structures we see are:

- 1 educational piece per week (what your buyers need to know)
- 1 social proof piece per fortnight (real customer/project outcome)
- 1 authority piece per month (your take on industry movement)
- Reactive content as needed (industry news, regulatory changes, seasonal)

That's a manageable content calendar for a business without a marketing team. AI handles execution. You handle approval and direction.

The calendar doesn't die because the creation burden has been removed.
