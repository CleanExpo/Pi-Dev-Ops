# Synthex — Ideal Customer Profile Research
**Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-05-08 | **Prepared by:** BG-1 Positioning Strategist

---

## Overview

Synthex serves three distinct customer profiles. Each has a fundamentally different relationship with social media, but they share a common pain: social media consumes time they don't have, and the output rarely reflects the brand they've built. The platform that solves this — without requiring them to become a content strategist — wins.

---

## Primary ICP: Trade-Sector SMB Owner (ANZ)

**Label:** "The Trade Operator Who Can't Afford to Be Invisible"

### Firmographics
- **Industry:** Restoration, cleaning, construction, pest control, plumbing, HVAC, landscaping
- **Geography:** Australia and New Zealand, primarily metro fringe and regional centres
- **Business size:** 2–25 employees, $500K–$5M annual revenue
- **Tenure:** 5–15 years in trade, 1–5 years attempting to market digitally
- **Tech stack:** ServiceM8, Tradify, or similar job management software; Xero or MYOB; Facebook Business page; maybe an inactive Instagram account
- **Decision-maker:** The owner-operator, sometimes a working spouse managing admin

### The Current "Solution" — And Why It's Painful
Most trade SMB owners handle social media one of three ways:
1. **They ignore it** — and lose work to competitors who show up in search.
2. **They post sporadically** — a job photo here, a before/after there, no strategy, no consistency.
3. **They pay an agency $1,500–$3,000/month** — and receive generic content that doesn't sound like them, doesn't mention their service area, and produces zero measurable leads.

The agency model is the most painful. The owner can't verify value. The content sounds like it was written for a corporate. Their Facebook page looks polished but dead — no engagement, no calls. They cancel after three months feeling burned.

### Pain Points
- "I'm too busy on the tools to think about content."
- "The marketing agency I hired didn't understand our industry."
- "Our Google reviews are good but we're invisible on social."
- "I know I should be posting but I don't know what to say."
- "My competitor is all over Facebook and I don't know how they do it."
- Social media feels like a full-time job on top of the actual job.
- Fear of saying something wrong or looking unprofessional online.

### Triggers (What Moves Them to Buy)
- A competitor wins a visible commercial contract and credits their online presence.
- A slow month — the pipeline dries up and they scramble to "do something about marketing."
- A peer in a trade association or mastermind (NRPG, Airtasker Pro network) mentions a tool.
- A failed agency relationship — they want control back.
- A Google AI Overview or ChatGPT result for their service category that doesn't include them.

### Vocabulary They Use
"Leads," "pipeline," "word of mouth," "show up online," "I'm not tech-savvy," "set and forget," "before and after," "on the tools," "tradie," "residential vs commercial," "Google ranking," "Facebook ads," "testimonials."

They do NOT say: "content strategy," "brand voice," "omnichannel," "engagement rate," "ICP."

### Watering Holes
- **Facebook Groups:** Trade-specific groups (restoration, cleaning industry ANZ), local business owner groups
- **Industry bodies:** IICRC events, NRPG / RestoreAssist community, Master Builders Association
- **Podcasts:** Trade business podcasts (The Tradie Diaries, Built to Grow)
- **YouTube:** "How to get more leads as a tradie" search queries
- **LinkedIn:** Lurking, not posting — they watch industry peers
- **In-person:** Trade expos, supplier events, franchise conferences

### Objections
- "I've been burned by marketing agencies before."
- "I don't have time to set this up."
- "Will it sound like me, or like a robot?"
- "Do I need to be on all those platforms? I only care about Facebook."
- "What if it posts something wrong about my business?"
- "My customers don't care about Instagram."
- "How much time will this actually take me each week?"

### Synthex Fit
This ICP needs one thing above all else: a system that runs without them. The CCW proof point (70% setup time reduction) is the killer stat for this persona. They are not skeptical of AI — they're skeptical of anything that adds to their plate. Show them the system does the work. Show them it sounds like a human who knows their business. Close on ROI, not features.

## Secondary ICP: Marketing Agency (White-Label Tier)

**Label:** "The Agency Operator Running Out of Hours"

### Firmographics
- **Business type:** Boutique digital marketing agency, freelance marketing consultant, or VA agency offering social media management
- **Team size:** 1–15 staff (often 1–3 core people + contractors)
- **Geography:** ANZ, UK, US — English-speaking markets
- **Revenue:** $200K–$2M annually from retainer clients
- **Clients served:** 5–30 active social media management clients, often SMBs in trades, health, retail, or hospitality
- **Current tools:** Buffer, Hootsuite, Sprout Social, Later — plus manual content creation in Canva, Notion, or Google Docs
- **Decision-maker:** Agency founder or operations lead

### The Current "Solution" — And Why It's Painful
Agency operators are caught in a margin trap. They charge $800–$2,000/month per client for social media management. But the actual labour — strategy, copywriting, scheduling, reporting — eats 10–20 hours per client per month. At 15 clients, that's potentially 300 hours of production work monthly. Margins collapse.

They've tried:
- Hiring junior content writers (inconsistent quality, high supervision overhead)
- Using ChatGPT for drafts (works, but requires heavy editing and no scheduling integration)
- Templating content (clients complain it all sounds the same)
- Offshoring to VA teams (quality control nightmare)

None of these solve the underlying problem: producing high-quality, brand-authentic content at scale without proportional labour cost.

### Pain Points
- Client content approval cycles kill scheduling efficiency.
- "Cookie-cutter" content gets clients asking "did you actually write this for us?"
- Reporting is manual and time-consuming.
- Can't take on new clients without hiring.
- Competitors are undercutting on price with inferior AI tools.
- Managing 9+ platforms per client with different content formats is operationally complex.

### Triggers
- Hitting a revenue ceiling — can't grow without adding headcount.
- Losing a client to a cheaper competitor using automation.
- A referral from another agency already using the platform.
- Discovering the white-label tier in a product comparison.
- A difficult month where content production fell behind for multiple clients.

### Vocabulary They Use
"Client retention," "deliverables," "content calendar," "approval workflow," "white-label," "agency margin," "SOPs," "scalability," "retainer," "platform management," "reporting," "onboarding time."

### Watering Holes
- **LinkedIn:** Active — this ICP posts and engages
- **Communities:** Agency owner Slack groups, Freelancer forums, Smart Passive Income, Agency Mavericks (Troy Dean), SMMA communities
- **Podcasts:** Agency-focused podcasts (Smart Agency Masterclass, Agency Highway)
- **Events:** Digital marketing conferences (Digital Summit, Semi Permanent)
- **YouTube:** Agency growth content, AI tools for agencies

### Objections
- "Can I white-label this for my clients?"
- "Will this replace my team or support them?"
- "What happens if the AI posts something that damages a client's reputation?"
- "My clients are in different niches — can it handle that?"
- "I've tried [Buffer/Hootsuite] and they don't solve the content problem, just the scheduling."
- "What does the client dashboard look like — can I show them this?"

### Synthex Fit
This ICP needs the white-label story and the efficiency numbers upfront. The 70% setup time reduction converts directly to margin. Multi-client management across 9 platforms from one dashboard is the product story. They want to see that Synthex isn't just another scheduler — it's an AI content engine they can put their brand on.

## Tertiary ICP: Creator / Influencer

**Label:** "The One-Person Media Company"

### Firmographics
- **Type:** Content creator, micro-influencer (10K–500K followers), personal brand builder, or niche educator
- **Platforms:** Instagram-first, TikTok-native, building presence on YouTube and LinkedIn
- **Income sources:** Brand deals, digital products, memberships, affiliate revenue
- **Geography:** Global, English-speaking
- **Team size:** Solo to 2-person (often with a VA)
- **Revenue:** $30K–$500K annually from content-driven business

### The Current "Solution" — And Why It's Painful
Creators are prolific on one or two platforms but spread thin everywhere else. They batch-create content on their primary platform and either ignore the rest or spend hours adapting the same content for different formats. The repurposing workflow is a grind: export from TikTok, reformat for Reels, write a LinkedIn caption that doesn't sound weird, remember to pin to Pinterest. Most simply don't.

The growth ceiling hits when they can't increase posting frequency without burning out, or when their primary platform's algorithm changes and they have no audience elsewhere.

### Pain Points
- Platform dependency risk — algorithm change wipes reach overnight.
- Posting on 9 platforms manually is a full-time job.
- Content sounds different on each platform because they've adapted it manually, inconsistently.
- Brand partnerships expect presence across multiple platforms — they look thin outside their main one.
- Can't afford a full social media manager, and generic tools don't understand their voice.

### Triggers
- A viral post on their primary platform that they failed to capitalise on elsewhere.
- An algorithm change that tanks reach and forces diversification.
- A brand deal that requires LinkedIn presence they don't have.
- Discovery via creator community recommendation or YouTube review.

### Vocabulary They Use
"My audience," "niche," "repurpose," "hook," "engagement," "algorithm," "brand deals," "my voice," "content bank," "batch day," "cross-post," "growth."

### Watering Holes
- **YouTube:** Creator economy channels (Think Media, Creator Wizard, Nas Daily)
- **Twitter/X:** Creator communities, build-in-public threads
- **Discord/Slack:** Niche creator communities
- **TikTok:** Creator tips content
- **Newsletters:** Creator Economy newsletter, Not a Newsletter (Josh Spector)

### Objections
- "Will it copy my voice or will it sound like AI?"
- "I don't want to automate the authentic parts."
- "I've tried [other tools] and the content was embarrassing."
- "My audience can tell when I'm not posting myself."
- "I only really need Instagram and TikTok — why pay for 9 platforms?"

### Synthex Fit
This ICP is won on persona learning and voice authenticity. The message is: Synthex doesn't replace you — it multiplies you. Your voice, your hooks, your patterns — trained into the system. They're skeptical of automation, so the proof is in a demo where they see output that actually sounds like them.

## Cross-ICP Insight: The Universal Pain

All three ICPs share one root problem: **they are time-poor people who know social media matters, have tried to solve it, and still feel like they're behind.**

The current market offers two bad options:
1. DIY tools that schedule but don't create (Buffer, Hootsuite, Later)
2. Agencies or freelancers that create but don't scale affordably

Synthex occupies the gap: **AI that creates AND schedules AND learns the brand** — at a price point accessible to all three ICPs. The platform wins when it can prove it does the work without removing the human.

The buying decision for every ICP is: "Will this finally take this off my plate, or will I spend the first three months setting it up only to abandon it?"

The CCW proof point (70% setup time reduction) answers this question directly. That number needs to be everywhere.
