# RestoreAssist Report Readiness Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before a restoration report leaves the business. It is built for Australian restoration teams that need the inspection, scope, estimate, and final report to stay connected to the same evidence.

## 1. Site Evidence

- Job address is correct.
- Date and time of inspection are recorded.
- Inspector or technician name is recorded.
- Photos are attached to the correct area or room.
- Moisture readings or relevant field readings are recorded where needed.
- Hazard type is clear.
- Notes explain what was seen on site.
- Any missing evidence is marked before the report is issued.

## 2. Scope

- The scope matches the site evidence.
- Each work item has a clear reason.
- The scope does not include unsupported work.
- Exclusions are listed.
- Customer, insurer, or property manager questions are noted.
- Any assumptions are marked as assumptions.

## 3. Estimate

- Labour lines match the scope.
- Equipment lines match the scope.
- Material lines match the scope.
- Regional pricing is checked.
- GST and totals are clear.
- The estimate can be traced back to the site evidence.

## 4. Compliance

- IICRC S500 references are used only where relevant.
- NCC 2022 references are used only where relevant.
- Technical terms are explained in plain English.
- No compliance claim is added without a reason.
- Safety or mould-related claims receive extra review.

## 5. Report Export

- PDF export opens correctly.
- Excel export opens correctly if required.
- Company details are correct.
- ABN and legal details are correct.
- Photos are readable.
- Captions and labels are clear.
- The final report is saved with the correct job name and date.

## 6. Before Sending

- The report answers what happened.
- The report explains what needs to happen next.
- The report shows why the work is needed.
- Unsupported claims are removed.
- Internal notes that should not be shared are removed.
- The recipient is correct.

## Simple Test

Before sending, ask:

"Could someone who was not on site understand the job from this report?"

If the answer is no, the report is not ready.

## RestoreAssist Workflow

RestoreAssist is built to connect:

1. Inspection.
2. Scoping.
3. Estimating.
4. Reporting.
