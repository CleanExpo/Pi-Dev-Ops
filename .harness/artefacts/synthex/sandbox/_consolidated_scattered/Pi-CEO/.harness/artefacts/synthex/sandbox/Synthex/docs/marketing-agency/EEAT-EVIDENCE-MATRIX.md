# E-E-A-T Evidence Matrix

## Decision

E-E-A-T is an evidence discipline, not a copywriting style. The system must show why a campaign is credible and block claims that cannot be supported.

## Matrix Dimensions

| Dimension | Evidence Examples | Campaign Use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Experience | customer story, field photo, implementation note, founder/client quote with consent | Demonstrates real-world use. |
| Expertise | qualified author, industry credential, technical explanation, process detail | Supports complex or professional claims. |
| Authority | recognised source, citation, award, partnership, verified profile | Supports trust in the brand/source. |
| Trust | transparent source, consent, licence, privacy handling, clear limitations | Reduces risk and prevents overclaiming. |

## Trust Score Inputs

- percentage of claims with evidence
- consent completeness
- licence completeness
- source quality
- recency of evidence
- author/expert attribution validity
- unsupported claim count
- high-risk claim count

## Claim Status

| Status | Meaning | Export Behaviour |
| --- | --- | --- |
| supported | Evidence passes. | Allowed. |
| weak | Evidence exists but is indirect or old. | Allowed with caution or softened wording. |
| unsupported | No evidence. | Blocked or rewritten. |
| prohibited | Legal, policy, consent, or safety issue. | Blocked. |

## High-Risk Claim Types

- health outcomes
- financial outcomes
- legal outcomes
- safety/compliance outcomes
- superlatives such as best, first, guaranteed
- comparative competitor claims
- quantified ROI/performance claims
- before/after claims

## Reviewer Output

The E-E-A-T reviewer must return:

- evidence matrix
- trust score
- blocked claims
- softened claim suggestions
- missing evidence requests
- client-ready decision

## Acceptance Criteria

- Client-ready export includes the E-E-A-T matrix.
- Unsupported factual claims are blocked.
- Fake authority and fake expert attribution are impossible by policy.
- Trust score is explainable from evidence records.
