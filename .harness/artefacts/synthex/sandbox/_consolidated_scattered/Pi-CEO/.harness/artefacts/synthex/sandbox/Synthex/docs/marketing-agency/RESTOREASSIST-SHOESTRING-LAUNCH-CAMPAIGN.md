# RestoreAssist Shoestring Launch Campaign

## Decision

Run the RestoreAssist.app launch as an organic-first, founder-led B2B campaign with tightly capped paid testing only after approval. The goal is not reach for its own sake. The goal is to put a credible workflow proof in front of Australian restoration operators, technicians, assessors, and property stakeholders with the smallest practical budget.

## Date Context

- Current date: 2026-05-16.
- Launch sprint: 2026-05-18 to 2026-05-24.
- Stabilisation and follow-up: 2026-05-25 to 2026-06-07.
- Earlier 2026-05-08 RestoreAssist launch material exists in repo context. This plan treats it as source material and re-launch fuel, not as the current campaign date.

## Shoestring Rule

Spend money only where it removes a launch blocker or creates measurable learning.

Do not spend on:

- agency retainers
- generic stock video packages
- broad cold paid reach
- vanity design polish
- expensive influencer outreach
- ad sets without tracking
- production assets without licence records

Spend only on:

- one licensed music track or approved audio source
- captioning/subtitle polish if automation is not clean
- a small LinkedIn paid test if the organic post proves traction
- a small Meta retargeting/engagement test only after explicit publish/spend approval
- domain, analytics, and landing-page fixes needed before traffic

## Launch Objective

Primary objective:

- Get qualified Australian restoration professionals to understand RestoreAssist in under 30 seconds and take one low-risk action: start with free reports, review the workflow, or request a sample report.

Secondary objectives:

- Establish founder and product credibility on LinkedIn.
- Build a retargetable audience from video viewers and site visitors.
- Collect objections from real operators and assessors.
- Create launch evidence for the next campaign wave.

## Positioning

One-line:

RestoreAssist helps Australian restoration teams turn verified site data into transparent, auditable reports across inspection, scoping, and estimating.

Short version:

Inspection. Scoping. Estimating. Connected.

Proof points allowed for draft use:

- verified site data
- transparent and auditable reports
- IICRC S500 compliance language where page/product evidence supports it
- multi-hazard support
- photo and data capture
- regional pricing
- PDF and Excel export
- free starter reports

Blocked until validated:

- guaranteed time saved
- guaranteed claim approval
- guaranteed cost reduction
- re-inspection percentage reduction
- industry-first or only-in-market claims unless evidence is attached
- any named customer result or testimonial without consent

## Core Offer

Primary offer:

- Start with 3 free reports.

Reason:

- Shoestring launches need low-friction proof. "Start with 3 reports" is concrete, product-backed, and lower risk than asking cold audiences to book a call.

Secondary offers:

- Review a sample report.
- Download the Restoration Report Readiness Checklist.
- Book an operator review.

Do not lead cold ads with:

- "Book a demo" as the only CTA.
- abstract AI messaging.
- broad transformation claims.

## Platform Plan

## Platform Constraints Verified 2026-05-16

Use these as planning constraints, then re-check before any paid launch:

- Artlist Enterprise API: documented for music catalogue/search/download and soundtrack pairing, not video generation.
- LinkedIn: Campaign Manager supports video, single image, carousel image, and document ads. LinkedIn currently documents a $10 minimum daily budget.
- LinkedIn video: video ads appear in feed; short videos under 30 seconds can loop until at least 30 seconds of playback.
- LinkedIn document ads: support PDF, DOC/DOCX, PPT/PPTX; lead-generation document ads support PDF.
- Meta: official video ad guidance supports video across Feed, Stories-style placements, and in-stream; Meta also supports lower-production formats such as slideshow and carousel.
- Synthex policy: Meta paid publishing remains blocked unless explicit approval and server-side gating exist.

### LinkedIn Organic

Use first because it costs nothing and matches the B2B trust path.

Assets:

- founder origin / why this exists
- 90-second authority explainer
- "From Site Evidence To Report" carousel
- sample report/checklist lead magnet
- operator review post

Posting rhythm:

- 1 founder post per weekday during launch sprint.
- 1 company page repost per day.
- 10 thoughtful comments per day on relevant restoration, insurance, property, and field-ops posts.
- 10 direct messages per day to warm contacts, each written manually.

### LinkedIn Paid

Use only after organic has identified the strongest asset.

Current official planning facts:

- LinkedIn Campaign Manager is the paid platform.
- LinkedIn publishes a minimum daily budget of $10.
- Video ads appear in feed and support full-funnel objectives.
- Document ads can promote PDFs, white papers, infographics, presentations, and similar assets.
- Single image ads support 1.91:1, 1:1, and 4:5 style planning, with vertical 4:5 recommended where useful.

Recommended shoestring setup:

- Objective: website visits or lead generation, depending on whether the lead magnet form is ready.
- Budget: minimum viable test only.
- Audience: Australia; restoration owners, building services, insurance assessing, property management, facilities.
- Creative count: 2 assets max in first test.
- Duration: 7-10 days.
- Stop if there are clicks but no qualified page engagement or replies.

### Facebook Organic

Use for practical, field-style education and remarketing fuel.

Assets:

- 30-second workflow cut
- 15-second retargeting cut
- static thumbnail posts
- short captioned walkthrough
- before/after claim-free process post, not customer outcome post

Distribution:

- RestoreAssist page.
- founder profile where appropriate.
- relevant owned groups/pages only if rules allow.
- partner channels only when the content helps their audience.

### Meta Paid

Default status: blocked until explicit approval.

If approved:

- Use engagement or traffic first, not conversion claims.
- Use one campaign, one tight budget cap, two creatives.
- Use 9:16 video for Reels/Stories and 4:5 or 1:1 for Feed.
- Do not expose publishing/ad-spend controls in Synthex unless future server-side gates exist.

Meta creative planning facts from official guidance:

- Meta supports video across in-stream, Feed, and Stories-style placements.
- Meta recommends short video for in-stream; 15 seconds is a practical cap.
- Meta supports simple low-budget formats such as slideshow, boosted posts, carousel, Stories, and video.

### Minimum Metrics

### Success Criteria

Minimum viable launch:

- 7 launch posts shipped.
- 3 core assets shipped.
- 50 manual outreach touches.
- 10 qualified replies/conversations.
- 5 product workflow reviews or starts.
- 0 unsupported claims published.
- 0 unlicensed client-ready assets exported.

Strong launch:

- 15+ qualified conversations.
- 10+ free report starts or equivalent product actions.
- One clear winning message angle.
- At least three useful objections for product/copy improvement.

## Stop Conditions

Stop or hold launch if:

- RestoreAssist.app has unresolved trust blockers, including placeholder ABN.
- Pricing or offer shown in ads does not match the site.
- Product screenshots are unavailable or misleading.
- Founder voice/likeness rights are unclear.
- Artlist/audio licence evidence is missing for exported video.
- A factual claim cannot be linked to evidence.
- Meta paid publishing is requested without explicit approval.

## QA Checklist

Before any asset is published:

- Brand name is RestoreAssist, not abbreviated in voiceover or title.
- No forbidden hype language.
- No competitor names in ad copy.
- Product is explained before CTA.
- CTA matches landing page.
- Captions are readable on mobile.
- Text fits safe areas.
- Audio licence is recorded if audio is used.
- Factual claims are evidence-linked.
- AI is explained through a specific workflow function.
- No fake testimonial, invented customer, or implied before/after claim.
