## The Honest Truth

We believe you should know what you're choosing between — not just what the marketing says.

**Buffer** is genuinely good for founders and small teams who want simple, clean scheduling at a low price point. If you're posting 5 times a week and don't need AI-generated content or AEO, Buffer does the job without complexity. Its free tier is one of the best in the market.

**Hootsuite** is the enterprise workhorse. If you're managing a large team, need deep analytics across 30+ platforms, and have a marketing department to run it — Hootsuite is mature, stable, and feature-complete. You're paying for that depth at $99/month minimum.

**Later** is built for visual brands — fashion, food, lifestyle, beauty. Its visual content calendar and Instagram-first design are excellent for creators who live in the aesthetic space. If that's your world, Later's UX is genuinely pleasant.

Where all three fall short: they're built for marketers who already know what they're doing. None of them help you figure out what to say. None of them are built for trade sectors. None of them do AEO. And none of them cost-effectively serve agencies who need to white-label the whole thing.
