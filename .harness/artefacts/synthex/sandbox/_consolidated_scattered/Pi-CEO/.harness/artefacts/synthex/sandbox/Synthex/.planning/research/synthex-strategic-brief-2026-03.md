## PART 2 — THE CUSTOMER

### Who they are

Small local business owner. Sole trader or 1–10 employees. Revenue $60k–$500k/year.
Types: tradie (plumber, electrician, builder), café, salon/barber, gym/PT, restaurant,
health clinic (physio, chiro, dental), local retail, cleaning service, landscaping.

**They are not marketers.** They did not go into business to run social media. They went into business
to cook food, fix pipes, cut hair, train bodies. Social media is an obligation they resent.

### What they actually feel about social media (in their words)

> _"So, you've taken on the role of CEO, customer service rep, and now… full-time social media manager?
> How's that going for you?"_ — Raven Digital Co (AU), 2025

> _"You wake up, grab your coffee, and tell yourself: Today's the day I finally get my social media
> sorted. You sit down, open Instagram, and… suddenly, it's three hours later."_

> _"Managing your own social media should feel simple. But somewhere between trying to crack the
> algorithm and figuring out why your engagement disappeared, it starts to feel less like a business
> tool and more like an unpaid, full-time job."_

### Their real pain points (ranked)

1. **Time** — 70% of small business owners spend less than 5 hours/week on marketing. Not because
   they don't value it — because they're doing everything.
2. **Not knowing what to post** — blank screen paralysis. Every day.
3. **Inconsistency** — they post in bursts, disappear, feel guilty, repeat. The word "inconsistent"
   is how they describe their social media failure.
4. **Algorithm unpredictability** — "What worked last week doesn't work this week."
5. **No ROI visibility** — posting without knowing what's actually working.
6. **Zero human support** from platforms — Meta account lockouts described as "a direct hit to
   livelihood" by Australian small business owners.

### What they actually want (not what they say they want)

They say "more followers." They mean "more customers walking through the door."
They say "better engagement." They mean "my phone ringing with enquiries."

**Outcome language that resonates:**

- "More customers walking through the door"
- "Phone ringing with bookings"
- "People finding you in your suburb"
- "Consistent presence without lifting a finger"
- "Know what's working"

**Language to AVOID** (triggers scepticism):

- "Omnichannel strategy"
- "Content velocity"
- "Engagement optimisation"
- "AI-powered marketing automation platform"
- "Grow your followers to 10,000" (they don't care about followers)
- "Our AI does everything for you" (sounds like a scam)

### Their objections (in order of frequency)

1. "I can't afford it" → usually means "I can't see the ROI"
2. "I don't have time to learn something new" → onboarding anxiety
3. "I already use [Canva/Buffer/Hootsuite]" → incumbent inertia
4. "It won't work for my type of business" → generic tools feel irrelevant
5. "I'll do it when things slow down" → never-coming future moment
6. "I've tried these things before and they didn't work" → past failure projected onto all tools

**Counter each objection directly in landing page copy:**

- Objection 1: "The cost of one missed booking per week pays for this"
- Objection 2: "Set up in 10 minutes. No training needed."
- Objection 3: "Works alongside what you already use"
- Objection 4: "Built specifically for cafés / tradies / salons"
- Objection 6: "Synthex is different because it sounds like you, not like generic AI"

## PART 3 — WHAT'S ACTUALLY WORKING

### Platform signals that drive local customer acquisition

| Platform                | Local Value                             | Key Signal                                                   |
| ----------------------- | --------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Google Business Profile | **Highest** — often the only touchpoint | Zero-click, high-intent searchers finding & calling directly |
| Facebook                | Very high (35+ demographic)             | Local groups, geo-targeted ads, community trust              |
| Instagram               | High (visual businesses)                | Geotags, DM shares are now the #1 algorithm signal           |
| Nextdoor                | Underestimated — highest trust          | 79% influenced by recommendations; 88% buy local weekly      |
| TikTok                  | Emerging, under-40                      | Algorithm still gives organic reach to local content         |

### Google Business Profile — the most important asset most local businesses ignore

- Businesses with 100+ photos receive **520% more calls** than businesses with fewer than 5 photos
- 42% of GBP searches result in direction requests or calls
- Well-optimised GBP drives **59 actions monthly on average**
- GBP Posts now influence whether a business appears in AI-generated local summaries
- **Almost no AI social media tool treats GBP as a first-class platform** — this is the gap

### Content that actually drives bookings (not just likes)

1. **Before/after and transformation content** — #1 for trades, salons, gyms, clinics
2. **Offers with explicit urgency** — "Book this week and get X" outperforms awareness content
3. **Behind-the-scenes / face of business** — authenticity outperforms polish
4. **Customer testimonials (especially screenshot + republish)** — repurposing Google reviews
5. **Job site / work photos posted same day** — real, immediate, authentic

### UGC vs professional content: the data is decisive

- UGC is **5x more likely to drive conversion** than professional content
- Customer engagement is **28% higher** with UGC vs branded content
- Consumers rate UGC **50% more trustworthy**
- A phone photo from a job site outperforms a studio shoot

**Synthex implication:** The business owner's phone camera is their most powerful marketing asset.
Help them use it. Don't make them feel like they need a photographer.

### Platform algorithm cheat sheet (2025-2026)

**TikTok:**

- Watch time is the #1 ranking signal — first 3 seconds are everything
- 3–4 posts per week is optimal
- Max 5 hashtags (more = spam signal)
- AI-generated content is being penalised — authenticity wins

**Instagram:**

- DM shares are now the #1 distribution signal — "share-worthy" > "likeable"
- 94% of Instagram distribution now driven by AI recommendations
- Geotag posts to suburb/location for local discovery
- Reels still outperform static for reach; carousels outperform for saves

**Pinterest:**

- It's a search and planning engine, not a social network
- Evergreen content lifespan (pins stay relevant for months/years)
- Idea Pins get the most distribution
- Works best for cafés, salons, gyms, home services

**Google Business Profile:**

- Post weekly updates — these now influence AI search result inclusion
- Every photo = a ranking signal
- Review responses are crawled for keywords

## PART 4 — AI ADOPTION LANDSCAPE

### The real AI sentiment split

- **82%** of small business owners think adopting AI is essential to stay competitive (PayPal, 2025)
- **66%** believe not adopting AI will hurt their competitiveness
- **But: 65.5%** worry AI will make their business "feel less personal or authentic"
- **83%** of consumers can now detect AI-generated content
- AI-generated content receives **5.44x less traffic** than human-generated content

**The paradox:** They know they need AI. They're scared it'll make them sound fake.

### The trust-building path for AI adoption

> One small win → expanded trust → broader adoption

The business owner who uses AI to draft one Instagram caption in 30 seconds and it **actually sounds
like them** will come back and use it for everything. The business owner whose first AI caption sounds
generic and robotic will cancel the free trial.

**This means:** The first-use experience is everything. Synthex must deliver a post in the user's
authentic voice in the first session. The onboarding flow that achieves this is the most important
feature in the product.

### What AI claims land vs what triggers scepticism

**LAND:**

- "AI drafts, you approve" — human stays in control
- "Spend 20 minutes a week, not 20 hours" — specific, believable
- "Content that sounds like you, generated in seconds" — addresses authenticity fear
- "AI handles the first draft; you handle the voice" — co-pilot framing

**TRIGGER SCEPTICISM:**

- "Our AI will grow your business on autopilot"
- "Never write content again"
- "AI that knows your customers better than you do"
- "10x more customers with AI"

## PART 5 — PRICING

### The sweet spot

**$49–$99/month** is the local business comfort zone for a tool that genuinely saves time.
Resistance begins at $150+/month unless ROI is clearly demonstrated.

**Reference anchors that work:**

- "Less than the cost of a single missed booking per week"
- "A social media manager costs $2,000–$3,000/month. Synthex is $49."
- "Less than a part-time employee earns in one hour"
- "A full-page local magazine ad: $400–$800/month. Synthex: $49."

### Current Synthex pricing (AUD)

- Starter: $49/month
- Pro: $99/month
- Agency: $249/month
- Enterprise: Custom

This is **correctly positioned**. Do not change it.

### Trial model

**14-day free trial (no credit card) + 30-day money-back guarantee** is the optimal combination.

- "Start free for 14 days" converts better than "Free trial available"
- No credit card removes the #1 barrier (fear of forgetting to cancel)
- 70% of SMBs prefer monthly billing — respect this

### Pricing gap opportunity

The market has a gap at **$20–$40/month** with real AI features. Buffer at $5/channel is too light.
Hootsuite at $99+ is too expensive. A future "Local Solo" plan at $29/month could dramatically
expand the addressable market.

## PART 6 — PRODUCT PRIORITIES (EVIDENCE-BASED)

Ranked by evidence strength from research:

### CRITICAL (must-have, directly addresses #1 pain)

1. **Voice onboarding** — train AI on the business's tone in the first session. Upload 3 old posts
   or describe your business in 2 sentences. First caption draft must sound like the owner.
2. **AI draft + approval workflow** — "AI writes, you decide" framing. Never auto-post without
   showing the human first.
3. **Google Business Profile as a native platform** — on equal footing with Instagram and Facebook.
   GBP posts are the highest-ROI activity most businesses aren't doing.
4. **Content calendar with industry-specific suggestions** — the blank-screen problem. Pre-loaded
   with "first week" content ideas specific to their business type.

### HIGH (strong differentiators)

5. **Engagement prediction score** — score every post before it goes out with one actionable
   suggestion. This is the #1 retention mechanic (FeedHive 95% satisfaction driven by this feature).
6. **Mobile-first "post from the job" UX** — take a photo → tap business type → get 3 options →
   post. Under 60 seconds. This wins word-of-mouth from tradies.
7. **UGC collection and republishing** — prompt customers to share photos/reviews, collect them,
   turn them into scheduled posts.
8. **Industry-specific templates** — trades mode, café mode, salon mode, gym mode. Content that
   actually fits their day ("just completed a job", "daily specials", "meet the team").
9. **Review response drafting** — AI drafts responses to Google/Facebook reviews. High-value,
   time-intensive, everyone knows they should do it, nobody does.

### MEDIUM (next-phase features)

10. **One-source content multiplier** — upload one photo/URL and get posts for Instagram, Facebook,
    GBP, TikTok, Pinterest all at once.
11. **Nextdoor integration** — almost no tool supports it. Highest trust network for local businesses.
    Genuine differentiator for trades, food, health clinics.
12. **AEO/GEO post optimisation** — automatically include location keywords, service area mentions,
    and local SEO signals in every post. (AEO/GEO research still compiling — see addendum)

## PART 7 — LANDING PAGE PRIORITIES

### Above the fold: what matters most

The current landing page has been significantly improved. These are the remaining highest-impact changes
based on research:

**Social proof must be industry-specific, not generic:**

- "Maria, owner of Bliss Hair Studio, Melbourne" not "small business owner"
- Ideal: one testimonial from a tradie, one from a café, one from a gym
- 4.2–4.5 star rating is optimal (all 5-star = looks fake to local business owners)

**The AI copy framing needs the co-pilot angle, not the replacement angle:**

- Current: messaging about automation may feel threatening
- Better: "AI writes the first draft. You approve, then it posts."
- Better: "Sounds like you, not like generic AI."

**Trust signals that work for local businesses:**

- Number of Australian local businesses using it (local = trustworthy)
- "No credit card needed" for trial
- "Cancel any time — no contracts"
- "Set up in 10 minutes"

**Comparison anchor to include:**

- Show a comparison with hiring a social media manager ($2,000–$3,000/month) or an agency ($3,000–$5,000/month)

### Copy to update

The current page is good but these tweaks would improve it:

1. **Hero subheadline** — add specificity: "Synthex writes and posts for your café, salon, gym,
   or trade business — so you can focus on running it."
2. **Social proof strip** — change "10,000+" to industry-specific "5,000+ cafés, salons, tradies
   and local businesses" to make it feel relevant
3. **How It Works** — Step 1 should mention voice training: "Tell us about your business and how
