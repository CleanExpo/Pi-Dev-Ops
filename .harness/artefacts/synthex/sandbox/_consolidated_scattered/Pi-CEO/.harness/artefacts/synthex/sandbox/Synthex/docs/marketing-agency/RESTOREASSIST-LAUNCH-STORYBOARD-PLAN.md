## Client-First Strategy

Client-first means the opening frame names the viewer's job problem before the product appears. RestoreAssist earns attention by reducing report friction, claim ambiguity, and documentation gaps.

Principles:

- Lead with the restoration professional's burden: job evidence, reports, insurer questions, rework, and time pressure.
- Introduce RestoreAssist only after the viewer sees their own workflow problem.
- Use proof language: verified site data, auditable reports, compliance, export, regional pricing.
- Avoid outcome claims that need unavailable evidence, such as guaranteed claim approval, guaranteed time saved, or guaranteed cost reduction.
- Avoid urgency copy. The audience already works inside urgent jobs.
- Never make up customer stories, testimonials, before/after results, or fake screenshots.

## Master Storyline

Working title: "One Job. One Record. One Report."

Narrative arc:

1. A restoration job starts with pressure and scattered facts.
2. The field team captures site evidence.
3. The office needs a report that insurers and clients can understand.
4. RestoreAssist connects inspection, scoping, and estimating.
5. The output is a transparent, auditable report built from verified site data.
6. The viewer is invited to start with a small, low-risk action.

Strategic intent:

- Show the viewer their current workflow first.
- Present RestoreAssist as a practical assistant, not a magic replacement.
- Make compliance and evidence feel operational, not bureaucratic.
- Position the app as Australian and restoration-specific.

## Audio Plan

Voice:

- Primary: Australian male founder-style voice if consent and voice rights exist.
- Fallback: licensed Australian narrator with calm operational delivery.
- Pace: 125-140 words per minute for authority explainer; 145-160 words per minute for short Facebook cuts.
- Delivery: direct, grounded, no hype, no urgency.

Artlist soundtrack search brief:

- Mood/category targets: business, technology, hopeful, corporate, commercial.
- Vocal type: instrumental.
- BPM: 85-115 for LinkedIn authority; 105-130 for Facebook short cuts.
- Duration: 30-120 seconds depending on cut.
- Avoid: cinematic trailer swells, aggressive percussion, inspirational overstatement.

Sound design:

- Sweep transition sound for RestoreAssist motion signature.
- Soft mono confirmation ticks for evidence/compliance chips.
- No alarm sounds, sirens, disaster ambience, or emotional manipulation.

Licence gate:

- No Artlist track enters client-ready export without song ID, name, artist, duration, BPM, categories, source URL or download URL, and licence evidence.
