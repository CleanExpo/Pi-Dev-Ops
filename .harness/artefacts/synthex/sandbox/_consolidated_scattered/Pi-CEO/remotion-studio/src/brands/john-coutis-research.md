## Voice & tone

### Tone descriptors (extracted from his + bureau copy)
- **Humorous** — "uses humour to communicate some of life's most important lessons"
- **Vulnerable** — "raw honesty" is the most-repeated phrase across bureau bios
- **Motivational** — pace and structure of a keynote, not a podcast monologue
- **Direct (Australian colloquial)** — "Never give up mate" attributed (Wayne Bennett quoting JC's posture)
- **Warm** — testimonials use words "happy attitude", "positive outlook", "great communicator"

### Themes that recur in every bio
1. Resilience / overcoming adversity
2. Mental health & wellbeing
3. Inclusion & disability advocacy
4. Anti-bullying (especially in school engagements)
5. "Treat people the way you wish to be treated" — listed as a stand-alone topic by Celebrity Speakers AU
6. Goal-setting
7. Human potential / possibility

### Signature phrases (verbatim where possible)
- "Half a Body, Full of Life" (master tagline — appears on homepage hero, used by Alliance Community as feature title)
- "No Legs No Limits" (LinkedIn headline + likely book title — confirm)
- "Rolling Success" (his podcast title — implies success-while-on-his-skateboard)
- "Never give up mate" (attributed by Wayne Bennett — Aussie idiom, fits his cadence)

### Forbidden territory (deduced from his message + audience sensitivity)
- Anything that frames his life as tragedy first (he insists on agency and humour first)
- Pity language ("poor", "unfortunate", "suffers from")
- Medical / clinical framing of his disability (he frames it as a starting point, not a condition)
- Sensationalist or shock language ("amazing", "incredible", "miraculous" — overused stereotypes for disability speakers)
- Generic motivational filler ("unlock your potential", "live your best life" — these are over-used in adjacent category and dilute his earned authority)
- Negation of disability identity ("inspite of", "despite his disability") — his framing is "with", not "despite"

## Audience demographics

### Primary (highest revenue + most-booked)
**Corporate Australia + Asia-Pacific** — leadership offsites, sales kick-offs, all-hands, resilience training. Sample named clients (from bureau bios): Australia Post, Telstra, Westpac, Roche Group, HCF, Beechworth Bakery, George Patterson Bates, NRL/Rugby League teams (Balmain Tigers, Wallabies under Rod McQueen, Dolphins under Wayne Bennett).

### Secondary
**Schools (K–12)** — 4,000+ schools nationally and internationally per Celebrity Speakers. Anti-bullying, inclusion, resilience. National Young Leaders Day circuit.

### Tertiary
**Sports teams** — pre-finals motivational keynote. Strong historical fit: rugby league, rugby union, cricket. (He himself was Australian Disabled Table Tennis Champion + competed in indoor + grade cricket.)

### Fit with NRPG / industry association thesis
- NRPG (expanded to all property services trades) targets **tradespeople, restoration crews, cleaners, technicians** — overwhelmingly male, ages 25–55, often early school leavers, often working through their own resilience / mental-health pressures.
- John's voice (humour + grit + Aussie + lived-experience-of-real-hardship) lands harder with this audience than the typical corporate-MBA-coach motivational speaker.
- His existing topic of **mental health for tradies** (implicit in his keynote menu) is a direct match for an industry association whose biggest unaddressed problem is suicide / drug / alcohol rates among trades.
- His "Treat people the way you wish to be treated" topic is a direct anti-bullying / workplace-culture asset NRPG can deploy to member firms.
- **Verdict: STRONG FIT.** His brand needs minor tuning (more "trades" framing, less "corporate keynote stage" framing) but the underlying voice is exactly what the association wants to sound like in front of trades.

## Risks / open questions

1. **Consent for content cadence**: How many YouTube episodes per month can he record? At 25+ years of speaking, he can monologue indefinitely — but on-camera production is a different muscle. <!-- TODO ask: weekly possible, fortnightly safe? -->
2. **Tone alignment with NRPG audience**: Trades audience prefers raw / sweary / no-filter Aussie tone. JC's school+corporate keynote tone may be slightly cleaner. Negotiate: episode tier-1 (general audience) stays clean, episode tier-2 (member-only) can lean rougher.
3. **Existing speaker-bureau exclusivities**: Six bureaus list him. None appear exclusive, but verify there is no clause that prevents him being titled "spokesman" of a third-party association.
4. **Rights to use his name + likeness in association marketing**: Standard talent agreement needed. Specify channels (YouTube, podcast, web, print, conference promo) + term + remuneration.
5. **OAM use**: He can use OAM post-nominal in association capacity; check that nothing in the protocol restricts his use of the medal in promotional / commercial settings (it usually does not, but Order of Australia guidance is worth a one-line confirmation).
6. **Insurance for travel / on-site conference work**: Confirm public liability + travel cover, especially for member-firm site visits.
