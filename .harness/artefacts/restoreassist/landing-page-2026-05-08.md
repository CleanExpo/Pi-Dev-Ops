## 2. PROBLEM SECTION

### The Managed Repair Lie

The panel gives you work. The TPA sets the rate. The adjuster approves the scope. And somehow at the end of a 40-hour job you're arguing over line items you already completed.

That is not a partnership. That is a system designed to transfer your margin to someone who never set foot on the loss.

**Pain Point 1: You Are Pricing to Their Schedule, Not Your Assessment**
Managed repair rate cards are not based on what restoration costs. They are based on what insurers have negotiated away from contractors who needed the volume. Every job you price to their template is a job where you're leaving money on the table — and validating a system that compresses your scope by design.

**Pain Point 2: Your Documentation Doesn't Hold Up**
Phone photos. A Word doc. Notes you typed up after the job. When the adjuster disputes your invoice — and they will — your documentation is informal and theirs is structured. Informal loses. Every time.

**Pain Point 3: The Property Owner Doesn't Know Who You Work For**
In a managed repair job, the homeowner thinks you are their contractor. You know you're operating inside an insurer's system. That gap is where your reputation lives. When the scope gets cut and the job doesn't get done right, you're the one standing in their lounge explaining it.

## 8. FAQ

**Q: Does RestoreAssist connect to insurer portals or TPA systems?**
No. RestoreAssist produces documentation for your records and your invoices. It does not interface with managed repair systems, insurer portals, or TPA platforms. If you need to submit documentation to an insurer, you export it from RestoreAssist and submit it yourself — on your terms, not theirs.

**Q: Is this built for the Australian and New Zealand market specifically?**
Yes. The workflows, terminology, and documentation standards inside RestoreAssist are built around ANZ restoration practice. This is not a US product adapted for ANZ — it was built for the ANZ industry from the start, as part of the NRPG ecosystem.

**Q: I'm already using Xactimate. Why would I switch?**
You probably would not switch entirely — but understand what Xactimate is. It is an adjuster's tool. It prices work to Xactimate schedules, which in ANZ frequently compress legitimate scope. RestoreAssist is your documentation and invoicing system, built around your professional assessment. Some contractors run both: Xactimate for insurer communication, RestoreAssist for their own records and independent invoicing.

**Q: Do I have to be part of NRPG to use RestoreAssist?**
No. You can download and use RestoreAssist independently. But NRPG membership is included in the subscription, and the community is where the real value compounds — practitioner knowledge, positioning support, and a network of independent contractors who have built what you are building.
