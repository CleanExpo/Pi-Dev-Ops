# RestoreAssist — ICP Research
**Voice: Klark Brown | Structure → Systems → Scale**

---

## ICP 1 — Primary: The Frustrated Panel Restorer

### Firmographics
- **Business size:** 2–8 staff (owner-operator + 1–3 techs, maybe an admin)
- **Revenue:** $400K–$1.2M AUD/NZD annually
- **Geography:** QLD, NSW, VIC — suburban and regional
- **Years in business:** 3–10 years
- **Panel status:** Currently on 2–4 managed repair panels (IAG, Suncorp, Allianz preferred suppliers or similar)
- **Tech stack:** Pen and paper, WhatsApp photos, maybe Xero for invoicing. Nothing for job documentation.
- **Certifications:** IICRC WRT, possibly ASD. Keeping up to stay on panels.

### The Daily Frustration (Their Exact Words)
- "I did the work. I have photos. The adjuster's saying it's not in scope. It was absolutely in scope."
- "We're sitting on $80K of completed jobs waiting to get paid. I can't make payroll off invoices."
- "They send their own assessor two weeks after we've dried it out. Of course it looks fine now."
- "I spend more time arguing about documentation than I do doing restoration."
- "The panel tells me the job is approved, I mobilise the crew, then the claim officer overrides it. No warning."
- "We lose money on panel jobs. We stay on them because we need the volume."

### Trigger Event
One specific bad adjuster experience — scope gets slashed on a commercial drying job, they eat $12K. They go searching for how to fight back. Or they hear a peer at an NRPG meetup say: "Mate, I got off the panels. Best decision I ever made."

### What They Google
- "how to dispute insurance scope restoration"
- "water damage documentation app Australia"
- "IICRC drying logs software"
- "restoration contractor not getting paid insurance"
- "moisture mapping software Australia"
- "how to get off insurance panels Australia restoration"
- "restoration business software small contractor"

### What Convinces Them
- Peer proof. If another 6-person QLD shop got off panels using RestoreAssist, that story closes them.
- A real dollar figure: "Recovered $X in disputed scopes in 90 days."
- Seeing the documentation format — something that looks like it would hold up in a dispute.
- Speed of setup. They're skeptical of software because they've been burned by complicated tools they abandoned.
- A demo that shows their exact scenario: the adjuster pushes back, here's what you have ready.

### Objections
- "We don't have time to learn new software mid-job."
- "The panels will drop us if we push back too hard." *(fear of retaliation)*
- "We tried [software] and it was too complicated."
- "Our jobs are all different — I don't see how a template covers it."
- "My techs won't use it. I can barely get them to fill in a job sheet."

## ICP 2 — Secondary: The Scaling Restorer Getting Off Panels

### Firmographics
- **Business size:** 10–25 staff (owner + ops manager, 3–6 techs, 1–2 admin/estimators)
- **Revenue:** $1.5M–$4M AUD/NZD annually
- **Geography:** Major metro — Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, Auckland, Perth
- **Years in business:** 5–15 years
- **Panel status:** Actively reducing panel dependency — targeting direct insurer relationships, strata managers, property managers, commercial clients
- **Tech stack:** Likely using Buildertrend, simPRO, or Ascora for project management. Xero or MYOB. But documentation is still fragmented — photos in Dropbox, logs in email, reports in Word.
- **Certifications:** IICRC full certification pathway. Multiple staff with WRT, CDS, possibly AMRT.

### The Daily Frustration (Their Exact Words)
- "We've got the work. We don't have the systems to handle the volume without errors."
- "My project manager is good but he's doing everything in his head. That doesn't scale."
- "We had a scope dispute last quarter and we couldn't find half the documentation. It was embarrassing."
- "Strata managers want a professional report. We're sending them a Word doc with photos pasted in."
- "I want to hire two more techs but I don't trust the documentation quality."
- "We've built the business. Now I need to build the business behind the business."

### Trigger Event
They hit a capacity ceiling — a job goes sideways because a tech didn't document properly, or they lose a commercial account because their reporting looks amateur compared to a competitor. Or they start the conversation with a financial advisor about valuing the business and realise systems = valuation.

### What They Google
- "restoration business management software Australia"
- "IICRC compliant documentation system"
- "water damage report software professional"
- "restoration company SOPs"
- "how to systemize a restoration business"
- "restoration contractor compliance software ANZ"
- "scaling a restoration business Australia"

### What Convinces Them
- A demo that maps to their operational reality — multiple techs, multiple jobs, manager oversight.
- Integration story: does it talk to Xero? Buildertrend? MYOB?
- Compliance credibility — that reports will hold up under IICRC audits, insurer reviews, or legal discovery.
- Case study of a similar-sized company that scaled from 12 to 22 staff with systems as the enabler.
- The pitch that their documentation = their asset base. A business with clean job records is worth more.

### Objections
- "We already have [simPRO / Buildertrend] — do we need another tool?"
- "My team won't adopt something new unless I mandate it hard."
- "What's the data security situation? We have insurer agreements."
- "We're mid-growth. This isn't the right time to change systems."
- "I need to see an ROI model before I take this to my ops manager."

## ICP 3 — Tertiary: The New Restorer Doing It Right From Day One

### Firmographics
- **Business size:** 0–2 staff (solo or with one helper/spouse)
- **Revenue:** $0–$350K AUD/NZD — early traction phase
- **Geography:** Regional QLD, regional NSW, NZ provincial, outer-metro growth corridors
- **Years in business:** 0–2 years
- **Panel status:** Deliberately avoiding panels — either philosophically opposed or warned off by peers
- **Tech stack:** Phone camera, Google Drive, maybe Tradify or ServiceM8 for scheduling
- **Certifications:** Fresh IICRC WRT. Studying for CDS.

### The Daily Frustration (Their Exact Words)
- "I know I need to document properly but I don't know what 'properly' means yet."
- "I've been in the industry 8 years working for someone else. Now I'm on my own and I don't have the systems I used to lean on."
- "My first few jobs I just did my best. But what happens when I get a dispute?"
- "I can't afford to get a bad reputation this early. One claim going wrong could end it."
- "I'm hearing horror stories from guys on the panels. I don't want that to be me."
- "NRPG guys keep talking about documentation. I don't fully understand it yet but I know it matters."

### Trigger Event
Peer recommendation in the NRPG community — a post, a conversation at a training day, or a direct message from a more experienced restorer who says "use this from the start, I wish I had." Fear of making an early mistake that follows them. Or they see a competitor's professional-looking report and feel exposed.

### What They Google
- "restoration contractor software Australia small business"
- "how to document water damage jobs"
- "IICRC drying documentation requirements"
- "starting a restoration business Australia"
- "water damage app for contractors"
- "mould remediation report template Australia"
- "what paperwork do I need for a water damage claim"

### What Convinces Them
- Simple, genuine language that doesn't assume expertise. Respect for where they are.
- "Built for restorers, not for insurers" — that framing matters to them.
- Price transparency. They're cash-careful. The value prop has to be immediate.
- The NRPG connection. If NRPG people vouch for it, that's the community signal they trust.
- A quick-start path. If they can document their first real job correctly within an hour of signing up, they're sold.

### Objections
- "I only have 2 jobs a month right now — do I even need this yet?"
- "Is this built for bigger companies? Will I be paying for stuff I don't use?"
- "I'm still learning the technical side. I don't want to worry about software on top of that."
- "Can I cancel easily if it doesn't work out?" *(low commitment threshold)*
- "What if I don't know how to fill it in correctly?" *(imposter syndrome)*
