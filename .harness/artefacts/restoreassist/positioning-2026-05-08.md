# RestoreAssist — Positioning Document

---

## Category Position

RestoreAssist is the documentation and invoicing platform for independent restoration contractors in Australia and New Zealand who are done letting adjusters dictate their scope.

---

## Core Belief

You work for the property owner. Full stop. Not the insurer. Not the TPA. Not the managed repair panel that takes 30% off the top and calls it "efficiency." The restoration industry in ANZ has been slowly handed over to people who don't do the work — and the result is contractors cutting scope, eating costs, and wondering why the numbers never add up. RestoreAssist exists because Restoprenuers deserve tools built for their reality, not tools built to make an adjuster's life easier. Documentation is power. Scope is your professional opinion. The invoice is the record of what you did — not what a carrier was willing to approve.

---

## The Problem It Solves

**Carrier and adjuster dependency.**
Most contractors in ANZ build their entire workflow around what the insurer will accept. They pre-shrink their scope. They soften their language. They wait for approval before they act. That's not independence — that's subcontracting with extra steps.

**Documentation burden.**
Photos on a phone. Notes in a text thread. Scope written up the night before invoicing. When a dispute comes, the contractor has nothing clean to stand on. The adjuster has a system. The contractor has a folder.

**Scope disputes.**
The managed repair model is designed to compress scope. When the contractor's documentation is informal and the insurer's system is structured, the insurer wins every time. RestoreAssist changes who shows up to that fight with a system.

---

## The Solution

RestoreAssist is an iOS app that puts structure behind your work — from the first site visit to the final invoice.

On-site, you capture moisture readings, photos, and observations in a format that holds up. In scope, you build line items on your professional assessment — not a pre-approved template. At invoice, you send documentation that tells the full story of what you found, what you did, and what it cost.

It is not built to interface with the insurer's portal. It is built to produce evidence so strong that disputing it is harder than paying it.

That is the difference.

---

## Three Positioning Pillars

### 1. Your Scope. Your Standard.
RestoreAssist is built around the contractor's assessment, not the insurer's pricing matrix. When you document with RestoreAssist, your scope reflects what the property actually needs — not what a managed repair rate card allows.

### 2. Documentation That Defends Itself
A photo on your camera roll is not documentation. RestoreAssist produces timestamped, site-anchored records that carry professional weight in a dispute. You built the case before the argument started.

### 3. Independence Has Infrastructure Now
The Restopreneur model only works if you have tools that match your convictions. RestoreAssist is the operational backbone of running an independent restoration business — part of the NRPG ecosystem built specifically for ANZ practitioners who refuse the panel.

---

## What RestoreAssist Is NOT

- Not a carrier integration tool. It does not connect to insurer portals, TPA platforms, or managed repair systems. That is a feature, not a gap.
- Not a generic field service app. It is purpose-built for water damage, fire, and mould restoration workflows.
- Not Xactimate in a different skin. Xactimate is built for adjusters to price work. RestoreAssist is built for contractors to document and invoice their work.
- Not a compliance workaround. It raises the standard of documentation — it does not help you hide what you did.
- Not for contractors who want to stay on the panel. If your business model depends on insurer referrals and managed repair volume, this is not your tool.

---

## Competitive Landscape in ANZ

**Nothing (most common)**
The majority of smaller restoration contractors in ANZ run documentation informally — phone photos, paper notes, emailed PDFs cobbled together in Word. When disputes arise, they lose. Not because they did bad work, but because they cannot prove they did good work.

**Excel and spreadsheets**
Some contractors have built their own systems. These work until they don't — version control fails, data is inconsistent, nothing is mobile-ready, and nothing produces professional documentation on-site.

**Xactimate**
Xactimate is the industry standard globally — and it was designed for the insurer's workflow, not the contractor's. It prices to Xactimate rates. In ANZ, those rates frequently compress legitimate scope. Using Xactimate means accepting the adjuster's framework from the first keystroke.

**Generic field service apps (ServiceM8, Tradify, etc.)**
These tools are built for trades broadly — plumbers, electricians, maintenance companies. They handle job management but have no understanding of moisture mapping, drying validation, or the specific documentation requirements of a loss event. They produce invoices. They do not produce defensible restoration records.

**Why RestoreAssist wins:**
It is the only tool in the ANZ market built specifically for the independent restoration contractor's workflow, in the voice of the NRPG movement, with documentation output designed to hold up in scope disputes — not just satisfy a job management requirement.

## The NRPG Connection

RestoreAssist lives inside NRPG — the National Restoration Practitioners Group — the ANZ movement for independent restorers who have chosen to operate outside the managed repair model.

This matters for three reasons.

First, the tool was shaped by practitioners, not product managers. The workflow reflects how restoration contractors actually work on loss events, not how a software company imagined they might.

Second, RestoreAssist users are part of a community with shared convictions. The NRPG network is not a trade association that negotiates with insurers. It is a movement that builds Restoprenuers — contractors who own their positioning, their pricing, and their relationship with the property owner.

Third, the independence movement needs infrastructure. Conviction without tools runs out. RestoreAssist is the operational layer underneath the NRPG philosophy — the thing that makes the stance sustainable day to day, job to job.

You do not just download an app. You join a body of contractors who have decided to do this differently.
