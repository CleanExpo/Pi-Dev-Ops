Scope grew 6x. Every line item held.

Failed mixer valve, single bathroom, leak across one weekend. Original scope: bathroom strip-out, kitchen ceiling repair below. Adjuster signed at $7,800.

Then the daily moisture readings came in. Hallway plate 36.2% on day three. Living room sub-floor 41% on day four. Mapped back to a saturated wall cavity feeding under-floor through the slab join.

Revised scope hit $47,000 — and stuck — because every additional line item carried its own stamped reading, its own dated photo, its own job code in the same continuous record. No retroactive note-padding. No phone-roll archaeology.

Adjuster paid without a second site visit.
