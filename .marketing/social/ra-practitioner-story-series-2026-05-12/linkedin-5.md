Lost a $14,000 mould job for one missing photo.

Cautionary one. Cat-3 mould remediation, Newcastle. Containment built correctly. Negative pressure verified. HEPA filtration logged.

What did not exist: a single time-stamped photo of the containment before demolition began. Just a written note in the job file — "Containment installed 0830."

Six weeks later, occupant filed a cross-contamination complaint. Insurer asked for the containment record. The note was there. The visual evidence was not.

Settlement: contractor ate the rework. $14,000.

A 9-second photo, embedded in the same record as the negative-pressure reading, would have closed the file the day the complaint landed.

What's the one piece of routine documentation everyone skips when the job is moving fast?
